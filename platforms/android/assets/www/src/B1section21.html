    • Grammar

    • Collocations

      to be to - arreglo para un plan futuro / tener que / haber de



      He is to get married next week Él se va a casar la semana que viene
      They are to get up early Ellos se tienen que levantar temprano
      Mike was to live in that city Mike había de vivir en esa ciudad



      to get one's own back - vengarse



      He made me cry, but I got my own back Él me hizo llorar, pero me vengué
      I don't want to get my own back because it's nonsense No quiero vengarme porque no tiene sentido
      Kate got her own back when she slept with that guy Kate se vengó cuando durmió con ese tío



      to be about to - estar a punto de



      Don't leave, please. Mr. Johnson is about to arrive No se vaya, por favor. El señor Johnson está a punto de llegar
      I'm waiting because I know Charles is about to call Estoy esperando porque sé que Charles está a punto de llamar
      I was about to leave when you phoned Estaba a punto de irme cuando llamaste



      keep one's word - cumplir la palabra



      You can trust him. He always keeps his word Puedes confiar en él. Siempre cumple su palabra
      I kept my word and took the children to the zoo Cumplí mi palabra y llevé a los niños al zoo
      You said you were helping me. Now you keep your word Dijiste que ibas a ayudarme. Ahora cumples tu palabra



      to be in charge of - ser responsable de algo / estar a cargo



      My father was in charge of the animals in the farm Mi padre estaba a cargo de los animales de la granja
      The teacher is in charge of the students in the class El profesor está a cargo de los estudiantes de la clase
      I'm in charge of my younger sister when we are alone Estoy a cargo de mi hermana menor cuando estamos solas

    •   Let's Go Camping

      Ralph has been promising his son, Theo, to go camping for some time now. They are finally settling in at the camping park. 

      Ralph: Well, son. I have kept my word. Here we are in the great outdoors. 

      Theo: It looks like it is about to rain. Look at those big black clouds over there. 

      Ralph: Don't worry. The cabin is waterproof. But we are probably going to need to find the macs, just in case. And you're wearing a sleeveless shirt. You will be cold. You should change into a shirt with sleeves and maybe an anorak. When it rains, it gets cold out here. 

      Theo: Okay, Dad. Did we bring the anoraks?

      Ralph: Yes, they are in the back of the car. I will be in charge of making the fire, while you get the anoraks. Could you bring my gym shoes, too? 

      Theo: Sure, Dad. I was about to ask if you wanted them.

      Ralph: I remember one camping trip with your uncle, my brother Stanley. I was to get up early in the morning for a swimming competition and your brother changed the alarm clock. I was really upset that I did not get to participate in the swimming competition because I woke up too late. 

      Theo: That was really mean of Uncle Stanley. What did you do? 

      Ralph: Well, I was so upset that it made me cry. But I got my own back. 

      Theo: How did you do that? 

      Ralph: Ha, ha. I put a snake in his cabin. Goodness was he scared. I laughed so hard! We ought to get to bed early. We are to get up early for a trek, remember. 

      Theo: Okay. But don't change the alarm clock. I wouldn't want to miss the trek.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
