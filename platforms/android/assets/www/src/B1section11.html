    • Grammar

    • Mustn't versus Don't Have to

      Prohibition

      Lack of Obligation

      mustn't

      don't have to

      Mustn't

      We translate it as "está prohibido". It should not be translated as "no deber". This verb means something is forbidden. It's typical to find with the subject "You”, it implies a general view when in Spanish we usually use a sentence with no subject.


      You must not smoke Está prohibido fumar


      At a psycological level

      "Tú no debes fumar” implies it's a moral level, but you can do it without an important consequence, it's your choice. However "Está prohibido fumar”implies the law doesn't allow you to do it or it's a rule in the place you are, so the consequence is an important one, it's a prohibition, it's not a moral choice.



      Examples:




      You mustn't go into this room Está prohibido entrar en esta habitación
      You mustn't use the telephone Está prohibido usar el teléfono
      You mustn't line up here Está prohibido hacer cola aquí
      You mustn't advertise on this wall Está prohibido pegar publicidad en esta pared
      You mustn't wait in this area, go to the waiting room Está prohibido esperar en esta zona, vaya a la sala de espera
      You mustn't play soccer in the park Está prohibido jugar al fútbol en el parque



      Don't Have to 
      We translate the verb don't have to as"No tener que + infinitivo”. It means you don't have an obligation, it's a lack of obligation.





      You don't have to help me today at home, darling, you can play with your friends Tú no tienes que ayudarme hoy en casa, cariño, puedes jugar con tus amigos
      I don't have to work tomorrow; we can go to the swimming pool No tengo que trabajar mañana; podemos ir a la alberca


      At a psycological level

      So it's a lack of obligation, but it doesn't mean you cannot do it. You can do it when you want.


      At home:
      - Mum: You don't have to clean anything, dear, it's fine.
      - Child: I'll do it in anyway, Mum, I don't want you to work that much.
      - Mum: As you like.

      With friends:
      - Henry: We are having a party at home next Saturday, are you coming?
      - Oscar: Yes, brilliant. Shall I bring anything?
      - Henry: No, don't worry. You don't have to. It's fine.
      * * Note: Oscar could take some beers, chips, etc. It's not a prohibition, it's just a lack of obligation.

    • The Freshest Ingredients

      What do you put into your body every day? Do you eat fast food? Do you use tinned food in your kitchen? 

      The foods you choose can directly affect your health. Of course, most tinned and bottled food tastes good and is satisfying, but you should choose fresher methods of conserving food in order to get the most vitamins and nutrition from the food that you eat. Tinned and bottled food is very convenient in the modern kitchen; however, tinned and bottled food has far less nutritional value than fresh food. These types of foodstuffs were created during times before refrigeration and continue to be sold today. With today's regulations, supermarkets must maintain tinned food only until a certain date; after that date, supermarkets mustn't sell the food. However, with fresh vegetables, if the vegetables are in good condition and appear to be fresh enough to eat, supermarkets do not have to throw them away. 

      You do not have to be a vegetarian to believe in fresh food; although, many vegetarians use only the freshest ingredients in their kitchens and even substitute sugar for other more natural sweeteners, such as agave honey and blackstrap molasses. Some health food companies have come up with mixtures of sweeteners that come from common plants. These are a good health choice in comparison with high fructose sweeteners. 

      Another thing to consider when talking about fresh ingredients is not just how fresh the ingredients are, but also how to cook them and retain the vitamins and minerals. Many people cook things like vegetables and fish too long and the result is that they are almost cancelling the nutritional benefits from their healthy choice of food. You do not have to cook fish and vegetables very long. Vegetables have to be simmered for a very short time, no more than 10 to 15 minutes, depending on the vegetable. By cooking vegetables this way, you can actually get up to 40% more vitamins that would have been lost in the cooking process. Choose fresh ingredients, but try to retain their freshness.
