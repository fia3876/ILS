      Notice

      These are the six basic sentence structures in English. We can add other elements after them asTime Complement, Place Complement or other adverbs, but they always follow one of these structures.

    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


      Katie's Umbrella

      Katie: Peter, have you seen my umbrella? I have been looking for it for 20 minutes. 

      Peter: I am not sure. What does it look like? 

      Katie: It is purple with yellow flowers on it. I usually have it here in the office for those occasions when it is raining, like now. 

      Peter: That one! I have seen it here before. I saw an umbrella behind the door, but it is not purple. Could that be the one? 

      Katie: No, that is Ryan's. 

      Peter: As I said, it is not purple with yellow flowers. Could it be that you forgot it at home? 

      Katie: No, because I have not taken it home. I usually leave it here in the office. That is the strange thing because it is really my office umbrella. But oddly it is gone. 

      Peter: Wait a minute. Now that I think about it, does it have a black handle? 

      Katie: Yes, that is the one. 

      Peter: I think I know what happened to it. Ryan lent it to a friend of his. 

      Katie: Why would he have done that? It is my umbrella and he has his own. 

      Peter: Well, he was leaving to have lunch with his friend and it started to rain. He only had one umbrella, which was his, so he let his friend use yours. He said he was going to bring it back yesterday but I guess he just forgot all about it. 

      Katie: I can't believe that! What am I supposed to do now? It is really raining a lot out there. I will be completely wet if I go out in the rain like that. 

      Peter: He will probably bring it back to you soon. Don't worry. 

      Katie: That won't help me right now, will it? 

      Peter: Why don't you just take Ryan's? He is the one that loaned yours. 

      Katie: I guess I could do that. Where is his? 

      Peter: It's behind the door. It is red and black with pictures of vampires on it. 

      Katie: Pictures of vampires? Goodness, I cannot believe I am going to use an umbrella with vampires on it. I guess I do not have a choice. 

      Peter: An umbrella is an umbrella. At least, it will keep you from getting wet. 

      Katie: I guess so. Thanks Peter. 

      Peter: No problem, Katie. I hope you don't get too wet.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
