    • Grammar

    • Phrasal verbs: make out, put off, use up, turn off

      make out -understand


      After his speech, I made out his true feelings

      After his speech, I made out his true feelings Después de su discurso, entendí sus verdaderos sentimientos
      I've made out the unit because you have helped me He comprendido el tema porque tú me has ayudado
      When he saw her behavior at work, he made out her problem Cuando él vio su comportamiento en el trabajo, comprendió su problema


      put off - postpone


      My boss has put the meeting off. He says we aren't ready

      My boss has put the meeting off. He says we aren't ready Mi jefe ha pospuesto la reunión. Dice que no estamos preparados
      The theater company has put the show off because the main actress is ill La compañía de teatro ha pospuesto el espectáculo porque la actriz principal está enferm
      The teacher has put the test off because he thinks that we need more time to study El profesor ha pospuesto el examen porque él cree que necesitamos más tiempo para estudiar


      use up - completely used


      I have bought new pens because I used mine up

      I have bought new pens because I used mine up He comprado bolígrafos nuevos porque gasté los míos
      My sister is buying sugar because we used it all up in the cake Mi hermana está comprando azúcar porque la gastamos toda en el pastel
      Tom needs some paper because he has used it up Tom necesita papel porque lo ha gastado


      turn off - repulse


      I was enjoying my dinner, but the staff's behaviour turned me off

      I was enjoying my dinner, but the staff's behavior turned me off Estaba disfrutando mi cena, pero el comportamiento de la plantilla me repelió
      The guy seemed nice, but his comments on abortion turned me off El tío parecía simpático, pero sus comentarios sobre el aborto me repelieron
      Wendy went back home because your manners turned her off Wendy se fue a casa porque tus modales la repelieron

      Political Speeches

      It is election time again and there are many politicians speaking on TV. They all have their own opinions and ideas but sometimes it is hard to make out exactly what they want to say. Politicians love to talk and make promises and this time of year when people are getting ready to vote they are all chattering away with promises and more promises. I am not sure if they know how ridiculous they sound. Of course, the people that vote know exactly what is going to happen in the future, but I guess they are just hoping for the best. Typically, once a politician is elected, all of the new committees, programs, ideas and promises are put off to the next year because they are not ready. Yes, they promise change, but not right now; it is better to put off those changes to next year or possibly it would be better to put them off for two years. 

      Some politicians are not as good as others when they speak. The ex-president of the United States, George Bush, is a good example. George Bush is not a great communicator and it is often difficult to make out what he is saying; in fact, most of his answers are confusing and difficult to comprehend. You have to ask yourself if he knows what he is talking about. Others are great communicators and their explanations and ideas are crystal clear and easy to understand for the general public. Voters are not politicians and we depend on them to communicate with us; after all, that is their job and why we vote for them. We all get one vote and we do not want to use it up voting for somebody that we cannot trust or understand. TV stations permit politicians to have a certain amount of time for their speeches; each candidate gets to use some time and should use it in a practical way before they use all the time up. Once it is gone, it is gone. It is all strategy to get the people excited about their position. 

      Politicians are careful not to turn off certain sectors of the community, for example women. Politicians know that with just a few words they can turn off all of the women voters. If they say the wrong thing, they will not have the support of that sector. There is nothing worse than turning off a whole group of people from listening to and accepting your ideas. When that happens early in a campaign, politicians often stop the campaign and put it off to a later date when people have forgotten about their comments


      Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
