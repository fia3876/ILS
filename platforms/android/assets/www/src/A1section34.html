    • Grammar

    •   El Artículo II: El Artículo indeterminado a, an. Usos

      1-Para expresar profesiones:

      She is a teacher Ella es profesora
      He is a lawyer Él es abogado
      I am a journalist Yo soy periodista

      Excepciones

      Existen algunas excepciones a esta regla, en las cuáles utilizamos el Artículo cero.

      He's Chairman of the Board Él es el presidente de la junta
      He's captain of the team Él es el capitán del equipo
      He's president of the league Él es el presidente de la liga

      2-En expresiones distributivas:

      Four times a week Cuatro veces a la semana
      Five pence a pound Cinco peniques la libra

      3-Para traducir la idea de"un tal”:

      A Mr. Jones Un tal Sr. Jones

      4-Delante de: cien, mil, million millón, docena, ...

      A hundred men Cien hombres
      A dozen eggs Una docena de huevos

      5-En exclamaciones introducidas por"what” (qué) seguido de un nombre contable en singular:

      What a nice dress! ¡Qué bonito vestido!

      6-En algunas expresiones idiomáticas:

      be in a hurry tener prisa
      be in a bad mood estar de mal humor
      all of a sudden repentinamente
      have a headache tener dolor de cabeza
      have a cold estar resfriado
      have a pain tener dolores, doler algo

    •   Ejemplos de uso

      What do you do? I'm a doctor ¿A qué te dedicas? Soy médico
      Bees come all of a sudden Las abejas vienen repentinamente
      What does your father do? He's a plumber ¿A qué se dedica tu padre? Él es plomero
      Who is his lawyer? A Mr. Smith ¿Quién es su abogado? Un tal Sr. Smith
      My brother is the captain of the school team Mi hermano es el capitán del equipo del colegio
      It's a quarter past five. I'm in a hurry Son las cinco y cuarto. Tengo prisa
      Why are you in a bad temper? I'm sad ¿Por qué estás de mal humor? Estoy triste
      I have a pain in my back Tengo dolor de espalda

      How often do you go to the movie theater? Twice a month ¿Con qué frecuencia vas al cine? Dos veces al mes
      Sue is ill. She has a cold and a headache Sue está enferma. Ella está resfriada y le duele la cabeza
      How often does your son study? Twice a week ¿Con qué frecuencia estudia su hijo? Dos veces a la semana
      How many milk bottles do you want? I want a dozen bottles, please ¿Cuántas botellas de leche quiere? Quiero una docena, por favor

    •   Hello Mom!


      How are you? How is the weather? I miss you. I also miss Dad and my sisters. I can't wait until school is finished so that I can visit! Everything is well here. I'm doing well in school. I LOVE MY CLASSES! I went to look for a job at a pet store down the street. My girlfriend Sarah works there. She also lives with me. She goes to a different school. She wants to buy an animal. I don't want an animal because I don't like pets.

      I have a problem. I do not feel well. I have been sick for two weeks. All of a sudden my teeth and legs hurt and I have a headache. You know I don't like doctors. Do you think I have a cold? I want to go to see the doctor, but I have no money. It costs two hundred dollars to see a doctor. I need your help. Can I have a hundred dollars, so that I can visit a doctor? I really need some help. I want to feel better soon because I want to play with the soccer team at school. I'm excited to play. They have a good league.

      Well, Mom, I'm sleepy. I have a class in the morning and I have an exam in that class. I want to study for an hour. Good night! I LOVE YOU!
      Love, Edward
