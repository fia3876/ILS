  • Lección 33.1: Modal Verbs in The Past Tense


    • Grammar

    •   Modal Verbs in the past

      You already know some modal verbs have a Past Tense:

      I had to study harder Tuve que estudiar más
      She didn't use to play with her brothers Ella no solía jugar con sus hermanos
      Meg didn't have to help me, I did it myself Meg no tuvo que ayudarme, lo hice sola


      The modal verbs that don't have a Past Tense can also express a past event. They do it by using a Perfect Infinitive, that is, they add a form similar to the Present Perfect after:

      I could have done it Yo podía haberlo hecho
      She must have worked a lot Ella debe haber trabajado mucho


      We always choose a modal verb in the past tense when it's available:

      present ability

      I can sing Yo puedo cantar


      past ability

      I could sing Yo podía cantar


      past perfect ability

      I could have sung Yo podía haber cantando



      It expresses something that is already done or undone, a past event that cannot change now.

      moral duty in the present

      I must go now Debo irme ahora


      moral duty in the past

      I must have gone Debí haber ido


      It expresses the same uses the modal verb has, but in a past time.


      Notice

      You SHOULD NEVER use"has” in these cases. Remember you can only change the first verb in a verbal tense. The first verb in this verbal tense is always the modal verb. Modal verbs NEVER add an -s for the third person. You cannot use it in the second verb:
      WRONG: Paul must has phoned you
      CORRECT: Paul must HAVE phoned you
      Paul must have phoned you Paul debió haberte llamado
      WRONG: Laura must has cleaned the house
      CORRECT: Laura must HAVE cleaned the house
      Laura must have cleaned the house Laura debió haber limpiado la casa
      Remember you are using a Perfect Infinitive and not a Present Perfect. You cannot add an -s to a Perfect Infinitive as you cannot add an -s to an to infinitive.


      When the verb doesn't have a Past Tense as it's the case in"must”, you translate it in the Past:


      I must make dinner now Debo hacer la cena ahora
      I must have woken you up last night. I apologize Debí haberme despertado anoche. Discúlpame



      Examples:

      I should have worried less. I knew everything would work out well Debería haberme preocupado menos. Yo sabía que todo saldría bien
      You could have washed-up Tú podías haber lavado los platos
      He might have hurt himself when he fell down Él podía haberse lastimado cuando se cayó
      They must have learned something important there Ellos debieron haber aprendido algo importante allí
      The girl could have fallen down La niña podía haberse caído
      William ought to have paid more attention William debería haber prestado más atención
      You must have helped your sister Tú debías haber ayudado a tu hermana
      They could have had an accident Ellos podían haber tenido un accidente

      A Police Investigation into a Robbery

      Angela Robles is a single woman that lives in a house alone. She lives in a nice neighbourhood and she has never had any security problems until Wednesday, when she came home and discovered that somebody had entered the house and taken some things of value from her house. She immediately called the police, who came to investigate the robbery.

      Policeman: Good evening, ma'am. I am Officer Philips. Can you tell me what happened here?

      Angela: Sure. Earlier in the evening, I went to my parents' house for Wednesday night dinner. It is a tradition that we have in our family that we always have dinner together on Wednesday nights. Anyway, when I got home, I could see that the door was open. I did not go inside because I was afraid that someone might be inside. I went to a neighbour's house so that I could call you.

      Policeman: Could you tell me what they took?

      Angela: Well, they took anything of value. I know that they took my DVD, the TV and the stereo, which were quite expensive. They also took some money that I had in the bedroom and my jewelry, including my diamonds. I am not sure if they took anything else. They may have taken other things, but I was so nervous that I have not checked everything, to be honest.

      Policeman: You said that you always go to your parents for dinner on Wednesdays?

      Angela: Yes.

      Policeman: They must have known your habits and decided to break into your house while you were out. It is very common to watch the owners of the houses in order to look for an opportunity. Do you have an alarm system?

      Angela: No, I do not. I probably should have installed one, but I could not afford it.

      Policeman: They may have come in through a window, but I am not really sure. You probably should not stay here tonight. You ought to stay with a friend until you can get an alarm system installed.

      Angela: That is a good idea. Thank you, Officer, for your time.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
