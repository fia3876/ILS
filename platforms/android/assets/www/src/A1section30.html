    • Grammar

    •   Los sustantivos, el plural II

      Continuamos estudiando el plural de los sustantivos en función de su terminación:

      Si terminan en -f o -fe la cambiamos por -v y añadiremos -es:


      knife cuchillo

      knives cuchillos

      shelf repisa

      shelves repisas

      half mitad

      helves mitades


      Excepciones a esta regla:



      belief creencia

      beliefs creencias

      chief jefe

      chiefs jefes

      roof techo

      roofs techos

      proof prueba

      proofs pruebas

      cliff acantilado

      cliffs acantilados

      safe caja fuerte

      safes cajas fuertes

      handkerchief pañuelo

      handkerchiefs pañuelos


      Admiten los dos plurales:



      scarf bufanda

      Resumiendo:


      General

      Añade -s

                chair   >    chair

               house  >   houses

                  cat     >   cats


      Termina en s, ss, x, sh, ch, o en la vocal "o" precendida de consonante

      Añade -es

      Tiene excepciones

                 patato   >   potatoes

                   bus     >    buses

                   box     >    boxes


      Termina en -y precedida de consonante

      Quita la -y, añade -ies

                   body   >   bodies

                      fly     >   files


      Termina en f, fe

      Quita la -f, -fe, añade -ves (tiene excepciones)

                    knife    >   knives

                    shelf    >   shelves

                     half     >   halves


      Plurales irregulares:


      Conservan sus plurales sajones, sufren una transformación vocálica para hacer su plural:



      man hombre

      men hombres

      woman mujer

      women mujeres

      child niño, niña

      children niños, niñas

      ox buey

      oxen bueyes

      foot pie

      feet pies

      tooth diente

      teeth dientes

      goose ganso

      geese gansos

      mouse ratón

      mice ratones

      dormouse lirón

      dormice lirones

      louse piojo

      lice piojos

      brother hermano

      brethren hermanos (de una cofradía)

      Ejemplos de uso



      My feet are small Mis pies son pequeños

      There are three shelves in the living room Hay tres repisas en el salón

      There are three women in the church Hay tres mujeres en la iglesia

      He has five swarves in his garden Él tiene cinco enanos en su jadín

      The roofs of the houses are red Los tejados de las casas son rojos

    • Reading

    • Reading: Lee el texto detenidamente. Puede que en el texto figuren palabras que todavía no se han estudiado, para estos casos aconsejamos el uso de un diccionario. Después de leer el texto varias veces, responde las preguntas que figuran del mismo.


    • The farm is fun. I'm eight years old, and I always go to the farm by car every week. Sarah, my friend, goes by train when it is cloudy. She has three rabbits (they're dwarves), and two mice at the farm. There are geese, dormice, hens, and horses at the farm. The farmer uses knives to cut the hooves of the horses.

      Sarah has many scarves and handkerchiefs that she takes to the farm. She uses the scarves to dress up like an Indian chief. The farm has many cliffs to play on. Sarah always bakes pies and cookies with her grandmother at the farm.

      The farmer usually drives the tractor in the morning when it is sunny. The cow goes to the pasture in the morning. The pig lies in the mud with her babies in the afternoon when it is sunny. The goats and chickens never like the cold weather. Sarah plays on the tractor at the farm.

      I always wake up early on Saturdays and Sundays to go to the farm. Sarah likes to feed the birds. There are two ponds at the farm. The ponds have goldfish, six carp, some snakes, several turtles, and three frogs. The farmer goes fishing with the dog when it is warm outside. Sometimes, he catches fish for dinner. I always walk with him to the ponds. He takes fishing poles, tackle boxes, buckets, and worms to the pond. He watches for rain clouds. Sarah always likes to eat fish for dinner. I never like to eat fish.

      There are always fun activities at the farm. The farm is never boring.

    • Lee el texto y decide si las frases son verdaderas (T) o falsas (F).
