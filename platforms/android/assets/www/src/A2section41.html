    • Grammar

    •   Bring and take III: Take

      También hay expresiones hechas con el verbo "take”: 

      take advantage of - sacar ventaja de


      He took advantage of his father's position in the firm and got a job there Él sacó partido de la posición de su padre en la empresa y consiguió el trabajo
      We aren't friends anymore because he wanted to take advantage of me No somos amigos más porque él quería sacar partido de mí
      Ruth took advantage of her trip and learned Japanese Ruth sacó partido de su viaje y aprendió japonés

      take for granted - dar por sentado


      He took for granted that you were at the party Él dio por sentado que tú estabas en la fiesta
      The students think the test will be easy, so they are taking it for granted Los estudiantes piensan que el examen será fácil, lo están dando por sentado

      take control of - tomar control de


      The kids are breaking everything. Can you help me to take control of the situation, please? Los niños lo están rompiendo todo. ¿Puedes ayudarme a tomar control de la situación, por favor?
      I need to take control of my life, everything is a mess Yo necesito tomar control de mi vida, todo es un desastre

      take your breath away - dejar sin respiración , sorprender


      The beautiful sunset took my breath away El hermoso atardecer me ha dejado sin aliento
      When I saw her in that dress it took my breath away Cuando la vi con ese vestido me dejó sin aliento

      take care of - cuidar


      Take care of your little sister while we are out, please Cuida a tu hermana pequeña mientras estamos fuera, por favor
      A thief stole my bike last week. I didn't take care of it Un ladrón me robó mi bici la semana pasada. Yo no la cuidé

      take part in - tomar parte en


      I have never taken part in a demonstration Yo nunca he tomado parte en una manifestación
      I didn't take part in the talk because they were speaking all the time Yo no tomé parte en la charla porque ellos estaban hablando todo el tiempo
      Moira took part in the race, she was the winner Moira participó en la carrera, ella fue la ganadora

      take place - tener lugar


      The meeting is taking place in the conference room La reunión está teniendo lugar en la sala de conferencias
      The class takes place in room six at seven o'clock La clase tiene lugar en la habitación 6 a las siete en punto
      The family dinner takes place in the King's Inn on Friday La cena familiar tiene lugar en el King's Inn el viernes

      take pride in - estar orgulloso de


      My boyfriend takes pride in me because I'm a good cook Mi novio está orgulloso de mí porque soy buena cocinera
      My father took pride in me because I finished my studies Mi padre estaba orgulloso de mí porque terminé mis estudios
      I don't take pride in you because you're a liar No estoy orgullosa de ti porque eres un mentiroso

      take in your stride - arreglártelas bien


      When he heard the surprising news he took it in stride Cuando escuchó las sorprendentes noticias él lo tomó con calma
      Mom always takes everything in stride Mamá siempre se las arregla bien

    • Reading: Lee el texto detenidamente. Puede que en el texto figuren palabras que todavía no se han estudiado, para estos casos aconsejamos el uso de un diccionario. Después de leer el texto varias veces, responde las preguntas que figuran del mismo.


      The world is a very diverse place and has different forms of government from nation to nation. Some nations are governed by a monarchy, which means that that they have a king or queen as the head of the country. Now, kings and queens do not sit on thrones wearing crowns all day. Those are the old days of the Tudors; however, there is an aristocracy that still exists in those countries and if you are born into nobility you will benefit from that all of your life. Emperors typically do not exist in modern times, but in other time periods many emperors ruled countries. 

      Other nations are controlled by dictators. These people take power of a country without a democracy and govern the country. Sometimes the citizens of the nation support the dictator and sometimes they do not. Dictators usually control the country until there is a revolution. 

      Many nations have chosen democracy and they are governed by a president. The citizens decide who is the best person to govern the country and vote for the best candidate. These countries seldom have revolutions, but revolutions do happen.
