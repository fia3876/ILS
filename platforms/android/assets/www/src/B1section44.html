    • Grammar

    •   False Friends

      actual- real

      present - actual


      Her present work implies speaking several languages

      Her present work implies speaking several languages Su trabajo actual implica hablar varios idiomas
      This is her actual name. She is lying Este es su nombre real. Ella está mintiendo

      carbon- carbono

      coal - carbón


      We have run out of coal for the chimney. Can you get some?

      We have run out of coal for the fireplace. Can you get some? Nos hemos quedado sin carbón para la chimenea. ¿Puedes conseguir un poco?
      I made a carbon copy of your drawing Hice una copia con papel carbón de tu dibujo

      argument- discusión

      plot- argumento


      My friends had an argument last week and now they don't talk to each other

      My friends had an argument last week and now they don't talk to each other Mis amigos tuvieron una discusión la semana pasada y ahora no se hablan
      The plot of A Midsummer's Night Dream is very funny. It's a fairy tale El argumento de Sueño de una Noche de Verano es muy divertido. Es un cuento de hadas

      conductor- revisor, cobrador, director de orquesta

      driver - conductor


      The conductor is coming. Where is your ticket?

      The conductor is coming. Where is your ticket? El revisor viene. ¿Dónde está tu billete?
      The bus driver was driving very fast. I was frightened El conductor del autobús estaba conduciendo muy rápido. Yo estaba asustada

      deception- engaño

      disappointment - decepción

      I don't like people that use deception to get their aim

      I don't like people that use deception to get their aim No me gusta la gente que usa el engaño para conseguir su objetivo
      It was a terrible disappointment to him not to get any news from her Fue una terrible decepción para él no tener ninguna noticia de ella

      firm- empresa

      signature- firma


      I changed my signature because the other one was very childish

      I changed my signature because the other one was very childish Cambié mi firma porque la otra era muy infantil
      I'm working for a new firm next month in Berlin Voy a trabajar para una empresa nueva el mes que viene en Berlín

      idiom- modismo

      language- idioma


      I love learning new languages. It's very interesting to me

      I love learning new languages. It's very interesting to me Me encanta aprender nuevos idiomas. Es muy interesante para mí
      The verb To Make has a lot of idioms El verbo Hacer tiene muchos modismos

      sensible- sensato

      sensitive - sensible


      My older brother is very sensible and responsible

      My older brother is very sensible and responsible Mi hermano mayor es muy sensato y responsable
      My friend is very sensitive. She always cries in the movies Mi amiga es muy sensible. Siempre llora en el cine

      genial- afable

      ingenious - genial


      Mark is a genial and caring boy. He's lovely

      Mark is a genial and caring boy. He's lovely Mark es un chico afable y cariñoso. Es encantador
      The principal's speech was really ingenious El discurso del director fue genial

    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


      The Firm

      Interviewer: In this law firm we only hire bilingual people, and in order to sign up as a candidate, you must fulfill the profile. I can see in your application that you come from Canada. Do you speak French?

      Candidate: No, I'm not from the French portion of Canada, but I am certainly a bilingual person. I'm as fluent in Spanish as I am in my native language English. I even know some idioms of both languages.

      Interviewer: Oh, really? How did you learn Spanish in order to get as good as you say? And when you answer remember: Don't tell me fairy tales. If I find out that you lied, I'll be disappointed by your deception, and your chances to get hired will not be good.

      Candidate: I'm half Peruvian, my mom is from Cuzco and my father is Canadian. During my childhood and early teens, I lived in Lima, Peru, because my father worked for the American Embassy there. Then we all moved to Canada, and I have lived there since I started university. So I was basically raised speaking both languages.

      Interviewer: Well, that goes onto the positive side of your chart, because worldliness is another asset that this firm always looks for, and you seem to have some of that. What is your actual nationality? Not your current or present location, because I know that is Canada. But what is your actual nationality? Do you have dual citizenship? That would be a real bonus!

      Candidate: Actually, now that you mention it, I do hold two passports, a Canadian and a Peruvian one. I'm actually very well-travelled, but now I prefer to get married and have a family; and that is why I am applying for a position in your law firm. I'll be building a solid career here, while travelling around the world.

      Interviewer: Well, the rest of your resume looks pretty impressive, and you seem to be a down to earth sensible guy. That is what we need in this firm, so let me present it to the committee. Some of them are very sensitive about hiring foreigners, but I think that you have a good chance at the position.

      Candidate: Thanks, should I ring you up?

      Interviewer: If you want, but I won't have an answer until Monday. Why don't you let me call you when I have news? If you are accepted, we will need your signature on the contract before you start to work.

      Candidate: Ok. Thanks!


      Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
