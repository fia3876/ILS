    • Grammar

    •   Semantic Field: Walk


       step - when you step, you move your feet to change your location


      I stepped carefully over the stones because I didn't want to fall down Di pasos cuidadosamente sobre las piedras porque no quería caerme
      He stepped into the house slowly because everybody was sleeping Él entró a pasos en la casa despacio porque todo el mundo estaba dormido

       

      wander       - you walk with no specific direction 




      I wandered around the park for a while because I wanted to relax Paseé un rato por el parque porque quería relajarme
      He wandered around the neighborhood to get used to the places Él paseo por la vecindad para acostumbrarse a los sitios



      tiptoe - you walk without putting your heels on the ground 

      Sandra tiptoed because she was practising for her dancing lesson


      Sandra tiptoed around the room because she was practicing ballet Sandra andaba de puntillas por la habitación porque estaba practicando ballet
      William tiptoed because he didn't want to wake his parents up William anduvo de puntillas porque no quería despertar a sus padres



      toddle       - you walk with quick, short steps. This is a word to describe children 
      My daughter toddles after me all the time


      My daughter toddles towards me all the time Mi hija camina a tropezones hacia mí todo el tiempo
      My sister can toddle now. She still falls down. It's very cute Mi hermana puede caminar a tropezones ahora. Ella todavía se cae. Es muy tierna


      pace       - you walk up and down a small area when you are usually anxious or impatient 

      My father paced all the time when we were waiting for the results of my mum's surgery


      My father paced up and down because he was nervous Mi padre daba vueltas de un lado para el otro porque estaba nervioso
      If you stopped pacing, you would feel better Si dejaras de dar vueltas de un lado para el otro, te sentirías mejor



      march       - you walk quickly and in a determined way. It sometimes means the person is angry

      She marched into the office and asked to see the manager Ella entró decidida en la oficina y pidió ver al manager
      My brother marched into my room and shouted at me Mi hermano entró decidido en mi habitación me gritó

    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


      A New Baby in the Family

      Two men are in a hospital waiting for their wives to have babies. For Dave, it is not the first child. He has a son that is 2 years old. But for Jeremy, it is his first child and he has no idea what to expect.

      Dave: So, Jeremy, is this your first child?

      Jeremy: Yes, it is and we are both really excited. But now I am getting anxious. We got here about four hours ago. At first, there was nothing for me to do. The doctors were taking care of Peggy, so I was just wandering around the hospital. But I started to get anxious so I came back here to the maternity ward, and I have been pacing around this room for the last hour.

      Dave: Don't worry. Most men pace when they are waiting for the baby to come. I remember with my first, I paced the floor for hours before he finally came! But when he got here, it was all worth it.

      Jeremy: So what is it like? You know, you're a father. What can I expect?

      Dave: Well, after all the excitement, the first two or three months are the most difficult

      Jeremy: Why is that?

      Dave: Well, you do not get much sleep because of the baby. Babies sleep for a few hours then they wake up. You have to wake up when they are awake; and you have to tiptoe around the house when they are asleep. It is strange.

      Jeremy: Yeah, you never think about the part when they are little babies, just the part when they start to walk.

      Dave: Oh, that part is great, but it is also the beginning of problems! My son just took his first steps the other day. He is not really walking yet, just toddling from one room to another. But you can see the potential for disaster.

      Jeremy: I do not know. I cannot wait for mine to take his first steps.


      Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
