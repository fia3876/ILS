    •   That Clauses: An Introduction To Relative Clauses

      We sometimes have two sentences as the following ones:

      I saw a woman yesterday. She is your neighbor Vi a una mujer ayer. Ella es tu vecina


      We can join these two sentences by using the word that (que) between them. Look:

      I saw a woman yesterday that is your neighbor Vi a una mujer ayer que es tu vecina

      Notice

      We always place the relative pronoun "that" instead of another person, an animal or a thing.
      I saw a woman yesterday that is your neighbor
      Vi a una mujer ayer que es tu vecina
       
      In our example we substitutes the subject (she) of the second sentence for the relative pronoun (that), which is working as the subject of the second sentence.


      We can use this word"that” after some verbs to introduce a new sentence:

      I said that it was a lie Dije que era una mentira
      He thinks that you are very clever Él piensa que tú eres muy lista
      I knew that this would happen Sabía que esto ocurriría
      They told me that you were coming Ellos me dijeron que tú venías
      Mike understood that it had been a misunderstanding Mike comprendió que había sido un malentendido
      My mom decided that this was her best choice Mi madre decidió que esa era su mejor opción
      My friends believed that they were right Mis amigos creían que ellos tenían razón
      It means that you'd rather wait here Quiere decir que mejor esperes aquí

      Notice

      If there is a person or subject after the word"that”. It means the word "that” is not working as the subject, but as an object.


      When the word"that” does not function as the subject, we can take it out:


      I said it was a lie Dije que era una mentira
      He thinks you are very clever Él piensa que tú eres muy lista
      I knew this would happen Sabía que esto ocurriría
      They told me you were coming Ellos me dijeron que tú venías
      Mike understood it had been a misunderstanding Mike comprendió que había sido un malentendido
      My mom decided this was her best choice Mi madre decidió que esa era su mejor opción
      My friends believed they were right Mis amigos creían que ellos tenían razón
      It means you'd rather wait here Quiere decir que mejor esperes aquí



      Remember this is just an introduction for you to be able to use the relative clauses and get used to them. It may be difficult for you to understand at the beginning, but we are studying them again in further lessons. Just keep these basic rules in mind.

      That Girl

      John and Brett are both single and have been invited to a party that is going to be at Sandra's house. One of the girls that John likes will be there so John is excited about going and Brett is going with him. John is hoping that he will have a chance to talk to the girl that he likes and get her name and maybe her phone number.

      Sandra: Hi, John! Good to see you! Where is Brett?

      John: He is coming. He will be here in about an hour.

      Sandra: I did not know that he would be late. Why don't you go and talk to my friend Tina?

      John: Which one is Tina?

      Sandra: She is the girl that is talking to Charlene. She is the pretty girl that is standing by the buffet. She is wearing a purple dress.

      John: Oh, I do not know her. Is that the girl that is a tennis player?

      Sandra: Yes, that is her.

      John: There is a girl that I really like, but I do not think that she is here

      Sandra: Which girl? What is her name?

      John: That is the thing, I don't know her name. But I would like to find out her name and get her phone number.

      Sandra: Is she the girl that is dancing with Steve?

      John: No, that is not the girl that I am talking about.

      Sandra: What does this girl look like?

      John: She's tall and has long hair that is curly and big green eyes that are similar to cat eyes.

      Sandra: Oh, I bet you mean Claudia. I am sure you are talking about Claudia, because she is the only girl I know that has big green eyes. Is that the girl that you like? Wow, I did not know that.

      John: I think so. Is she here? Brett told me that she would be here.

      Sandra: Do you see the girl that is going into the kitchen? She is wearing black pants and a red blouse.

      John: Yes, I see her.

      Sandra: Is that her?

      John: Yes, that is the girl! Thanks, Sandra, I have got to go. That is the girl that I have been looking for.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
