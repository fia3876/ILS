    •  Should, Shouldn't - Debería

      Should is a modal verb meaning "debería” in Spanish.

      Nota

      Should works in the same way as "can”. It is also an auxiliary verb, so it makes the negative and interrogative form of the tense, but as a modal verb it also adds meaning to the main verb, so we use it in the affirmative sentence as well.

      Here are some examples:
      - Affirmative clause: You should eat less - Tú deberías comer menos
      - Negative clause: You shouldn't eat less -Tú no deberías comer menos
      - Interrogative clause: Should you eat less? -¿Deberías comer menos?
      - Interrogative clause + an interrogative pronoun: Why should you eat less? -¿ Por qué deberías comer menos?

      Nota

      Most modal verbs don't change its form regarding the subject.

      Examples:
      I should exercise more - Yo debería hacer más ejercicio
      You should exercise more - Tú deberías hacer más ejercicio
      He should exercise more - Él debería hacer más ejercicio
      She should exercise more - Ella debería hacer más ejercicio
      It should exercise more - Ello debería hacer más ejercicio
      We should exercise more - Nosotros deberíamos hacer más ejercicio
      You should exercise more - Vosotros deberíais hacer más ejercicio
      They should exercise more - Ellos deberían hacer más ejercicio

      We use this verb to give advice:


      You should study harder Tú deberías estudiar más duramente
      He should work less hours Él debería trabajar menos horas
      We should hurry, it's very late Deberíamos darnos prisa, es muy tarde
      They should get up earlier Ellos deberían levantarse más temprano
      Mom should rest a while Mamá debería descansar un poco
      My brother shouldn't lie Mi hermano no debería mentir
      Lilly shouldn't throw trash on the floor Lilly no debería tirar basura al suelo


      Should I clean this mess? ¿Debería yo limpiar este desastre?

      Should you call your parents? ¿Deberías tú llamar a tus padres?

      Should we wait for your sister? ¿Deberíamos esperar a tu hermana?
      Esther shouldn't be late for school Esther no debería llegar tarde al colegio
      You shouldn't believe everything No deberías creértelo todo


      Should Tina wash the dishes? ¿Debería Tina lavar los platos?
      Should I tell her? ¿Debería contárselo a ella?
      They should be honest Ellos deberían ser sinceros
      Mike shouldn't be rude to you Mike no debería ser grosero contigo
      You shouldn't stay up late, you work early tomorrow No deberías quedarte despierto hasta tarde, trabajas temprano mañana

        Ask Doctor Lewis

      Doctor Lewis is a very smart psychologist that gives advice to people with problems. People write Doctor Lewis letters or emails about their crisis and Doctor Lewis publishes a response to the letters. 

      Dear Dr. Lewis:
      My name is Craig and I am 16 years old. I constantly fight with my parents because they will not give me permission to go out on weekends. They don't like my music or my friends. I want to leave home.
      Dear Craig:
      I know that it is difficult to be a teenager, but you shouldn't leave home. That isn't the solution to your problem. Remember your parents love you very much and they are just worried about you. You are only 16 and you shouldn't go out every weekend, but your parents should let you go out occasionally. You should talk to them and see if you can negotiate going out sometimes. When they know that you will be responsible, it is easier for them to give you permission.
      Dear Dr. Lewis:
      My name is Herb and I am having problems at work. I have a colleague that never does his work and he always lies to the boss. The boss really likes this guy and he thinks that the guy does good work. I am usually the person who does all of the work and when I tell my boss, he does not want to believe me. What should I do?
      Dear Herb:
      You shouldn't do your colleague's work. If you do his work, he is never going to change. You say that you talked to your boss and he did not believe you. You should talk to your boss's boss, but you should be careful because your boss can get angry if he knows that you did this. Another option is that you should talk to someone in human resources to see if you can transfer to another department.
