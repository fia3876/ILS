    • Grammar

    •   Bring and Take II: Take

      take - llevar


      Can you take John a message, please? ¿Puedes llevarle un mensaje a John, por favor?


      También tiene otros significados: 

      take - tomar / coger un medio de transporte



      They are leaving today. They are taking the afternoon train Ellos se van hoy. Ellos tomarán el tren por la tarde
      I don't want to take the bus. I want to go by car Yo no quiero coger el autobús. Yo quiero ir en coche
      They are taking the plane in the morning, they are flying to Greece Ellos toman el avión por la mañana, ellos van a Grecia


      take - agarrar / coger



      Lydia took the gun from him Lydia le quitó la pistola a él
      I took a book from the library Yo tomé un libro de la biblioteca
      The kid has taken some cookies from the kitchen El niño ha tomado algunas galletas de la cocina

      take - tardar



      The journey takes three hours by plane El viaje tarda tres horas en avión
      The test took four hours, I'm very tired El examen duró cuatro horas, estoy muy cansada
      The flight from Berlin to India takes fourteen hours, it's really long El vuelo de Berlín a India dura 14 horas, es muy largo

      take - coger / tomar una calle



      Go straight and take the second street on your left. You will find the drugstore between the movie theater and the flower shop Siga derecho y tome la segunda calle a su izquierda. Encontrará la farmacia entre el cine y la florería
      Go straight and take the first street on your left. The Church is on your right Siga derecho y coja la primera calle a la derecha. La iglesia está a su derecha


      take - tomar medicinas, alimentos



      I'm taking some pills because I have a cold Me estoy tomando algunas pastillas porque estoy resfriado
      July doesn't eat any fruit, she doesn't like it July no come nada de fruta, a ella no le gusta
      Do you take any sugar in your coffee, dear? ¿Tomas azúcar en el café, querida?


      take - aguantar



      Frank is very funny, but George can't take a joke Frank es muy divertido, pero George no aguanta una broma
      When we moved, the kids took it very badly Cuando nos mudamos de casa, los niños lo tomaron muy mal
      I only take medicine when I am really sick Sólo tomo medicina cuando estoy realmente enfermo


      take - hacer un examen, tomar un curso



      John took driving lessons and then he passed the test John tomó clases de conducir y él aprobó el examen

      I have never taken a French course. I speak Italian Yo nunca he tomado un curso de francés. Yo hablo italiano
      I took the test, but I think I've failed, I didn't study much Yo hice el examen, pero creo que he reprobado, no estudié mucho

    •   Letter from Selma


      Good morning, Mr. Bernstein

      I only have two more days of looking after my mother in the hospital. I am sorry it has taken so long for her to recuperate from her surgery. I was thinking that you are probably doing just fine without me, but I just want you to remember to do a few things. 

      First, do not forget to take the pills for your blood pressure. You know it is very important! Remember to take the children to the grocer on Friday because that is when they have the veal that that they like. Take about 20 Euros with you when you go. The veal usually costs about that. 

      You have a meeting on Saturday morning at the office near the greengrocer's. To get there take Surrey Street until you get to Dever, take a left on Dever and then take a right on Jonesbury. That takes you to the greengrocer's. The office is just the next street. 

      Oh, and remember to take the books back to the library or there will be a charge. 

      Well, with the new doctor I do not think I will take long before I am back in the office to organize everything. I won't be long! 

      Your secretary 

      Selma
