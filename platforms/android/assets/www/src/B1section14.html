    • Grammar

    •   Prepositional Verbs and Adjectives


      translate into - traducir a


      I cannot translate this sentence into French. Can you help me? No puedo traducir esta frase al francés. ¿Puedes ayudarme?
      I am translating a very interesting book from Spanish into English Estoy traduciendo un libro muy interesante de español a inglés


      worry about - preocuparse de


      Stop worrying about me. I know what I am doing Deja de preocuparte por mí. Yo sé lo que hago
      I am not worrying about it. It makes no sense No me estoy preocupando de ello. No tiene sentido


      pay for - pagar algo


      I paid for the meal because I wanted to invite you Pagué la comida porque quería invitarte
      Liza is very responsible. She pays her rent every month Liza es muy responsable. Ella paga su alquiler cada mes


      remind of - recordarte a algo/alguien


      He reminded me of my brother. He speaks very similar to him Él me recordó a mi hermano. Habla muy parecido a él
      My mother reminded me of my duties. She is very strict Mi madre me recordó mis obligaciones. Ella es muy estricta


      happen to - ocurrir que, suceder que


      My friend happened to be there when they were gossiping about me Pasó que mi amigo estaba allí cuando ellos estaban cotilleando sobre mí
      It happened to be true. I couldn't believe it Pasó que era verdad. Yo no podía creerlo

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.

    •   Kim Lee

      Kim Lee is Chinese, but she was born in Los Angeles, California. She grew up speaking English and a little bit of Chinese. The older she got, the more English she was speaking, especially in school. Kim's parents still speak Chinese and can also speak English quite well because Kim's mother studied English at a local language school for Chinese immigrants. Kim has decided to take Mandarin Chinese classes to conserve her Chinese language skills.

      Kim: Mom, can you help me? I am really anxious about my Chinese class.

      Mom: Why are you anxious? You are Chinese!

      Kim: Well, the teacher reminded us today that we must translate twenty pages of a book in English into Chinese and I am really worried about this project.

      Mom: Kim, I cannot help you do your homework; you must do it yourself. I am not paying for your Chinese classes so that I can do your homework for you.

      Kim: But if you can just look at it when I am finished, I won't be so worried about it. The problem is that the teacher happens to be very strict. It must be really good Chinese. Here, look at this book that we are using in class. I think that it is really hard.

      Mom: This book reminds me of the book that I used in school when I was a young girl in China. I do not think it is very hard. It is the same way that I learned Chinese. Just put your mind to it and you can do it. Start with the first sentence and translate it one sentence at a time.

      Kim: That is a good idea. Thanks, Mom, for the advice. Oh, and thanks for paying for my Chinese classes, too.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
