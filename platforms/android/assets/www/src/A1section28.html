    • Grammar

    •   La -s de la tercera persona del singular

      Para conjugar cualquier verbo en tercera persona (he, she, it) del presente simple, añadimos una -s al verbo:

      He drinks water for lunch Él bebe agua para almorzar
      She plays chess on Fridays Ella juega al ajedrez los viernes


      Hay excepciones a esta regla:

      1- Si el verbo termina en x, s, ss, sh, ch o en la vocal o precedida de consonante, añadirá -es:


      He goes to work by train Él va a trabajar en tren
      She finishes school at four o'clock Ella termina el colegio a las cuatro
      The cat washes its face in the morning El gato se lava la cara por la mañana
      He watches a movie in the evening Él ve una película por la noche
      My sister does her homework at home Mi hermana hace la tarea en casa


      2-Si el verbo termina en-e, seguiremos la regla general y añadiremos sólo una -s:



      Mom prepares lunch at twelve in the morning Mami prepare el almuerzo a las doce de la mañana

      Uncle Louis comes at Christmas every year El tío Louis viene en Navidad todos los años

      My brother drives slowly Mi hermano conduce lento

      The cat sees the bird El gato ve al pájaro


      3- Si el verbo termina en-y precedida de consonante, se cambia la -y por -i y añade -es:



      Mike studies English and Spanish at school Mike estudia inglés y español en el colegio

      A plane flies fast Un avión vuela rápido

      Ruth tries a new food once a month Ruth prueba una nueva comida una vez al mes

      John tidies his room in the morning John ordena su habitación por la mañana


      Si la -y está precedida de vocal añadimos -s


      She plays basketball in the afternoon Ella juega al baloncesto por la tarde
      Mom buys the newspaper on Saturday Mami compra el periódico los sábados
      Susan stays at a hotel on her vacation Susan se queda en un hotel en sus vacaciones

    •   Resumiendo

      General

      Añade -s

      • drink = drinks
      • read = reads
      • sleep = sleeps

      termina en x, s, ss, sh, ch o en la vocal "o" precedida de consonante

      Añade -es

      • go = goes
      • finish = finishes
      • wash = washes
      termina en -e

      Añade -s

      • come = comes
      • drive = drives
      • see = sees
      termina en-y precedida de consonante

      Quita la -y, añade -ies

      • study = studies
      • fly = flies
      • tidy = tidies

    •   A Police Officer Questions a Suspect's Mother

      Police: Good morning, Mrs. Smith. I need to ask you a few questions about your son. Is he here?

      Mrs. Smith: No. Tom always leaves the house at 7 a.m. on weekdays. He has to go to school until the semester finishes next month. 

      Police: Whose car is that in the driveway?

      Mrs. Smith: That is my husband Joe's car. He is at home to prepare his lessons. Police: Does he go to school also?

      Mrs. Smith: No, he teaches at the university.

      Police: Does your son Tom drive to class?

      Mrs. Smith: No, he doesn't have a car. He usually takes the bus in the morning.

      Police: What time does he come after school?

      Mrs. Smith: On Thursdays and Fridays, he finishes school at 2 p.m. Then he takes the bus home, or sometimes I pick him up. On Mondays, Tom has basketball practise after school, so he stays until 5 p.m. On Tuesdays and Wednesdays, he works at McDonald's and does not get home until 7 p.m.

      Police: What does Tom do on the weekends? Does he play in the neighborhood?

      Mrs. Smith: No, he never stays in the neighborhood on weekends. He often goes to the movies with his friend Bob. Once a month, he visits his grandparents in Chicago.

      Police: Look at this blue hat. Is this Tom's hat?

      Mrs. Smith: That is not Tom's hat.

      Police: Okay, Mrs. Smith. Thank you for your time. I believe your son is innocent. Please call us with any new information.
