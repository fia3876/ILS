    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.

    •   Vacation in England

      Mary: Ellen, I see you are back from your vacation in England. How was it? 

      Ellen: Great! I had a wonderful time. It was raining, of course, most of the three weeks that I was in London. 

      Mary: Where did you stay? 

      Ellen: Well, as you know, I have relative that live in London. I was planning to stay with them, and had sent them a letter that I was coming; but when I got there, they were on holiday so I had to stay in a cheap hotel for a few days. Once they got home I stayed with them. 

      Mary: You weren't afraid to be alone in London without your family? I would be afraid of trying to understand the British accents and all of that. Did you have any problems understanding the people? 

      Ellen: It was not easy at first, but by the time my family got back from holiday, I could understand what they were saying. The strange part was the words that they use. I was completely unfamiliar with them. 

      Mary: What do you mean? English is English. 

      Ellen: Well, yes and no. For example, did you know that a clothespin is called a clothes peg in England? And diapers are called nappies. 

      Mary: Wow! That is strange. Tell me some more. 

      Ellen: Okay. Let's see. Oh, yeah, sneakers are called pimsolls. 

      Mary: Why are they called pimsolls? Ellen: Because Pimsoll was a popular company that sold sneakers and everybody called them by the name of the company. Here's another one: a bobby pin is called a hair grip in British English. I even had to buy a British English dictionary. Of course, the grammar is almost the same and it is mainly the words that they use are culturally different. 

      Mary: That is really cool that you had a chance to learn that. 

      Ellen: Yeah, that way I am prepared for my next trip, and on my next trip I am going to take my mackintosh. 

      Mary: What's that? 

      Ellen: A raincoat.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
