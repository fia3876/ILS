    • Grammar

    •   Make and Do

      We are studying more fixed expressions with make and go in this unit:

      make a face to - distort the features of the face

      I was depressed, but my brother made a face and I laughed a lot Estaba deprimida, pero mi hermano me hizo caras y me reí mucho
      My friend is a clown. He is always making faces and telling jokes Mi amigo es un payaso. Siempre está haciendo caras y contando chistes


      make a go of - success

      He opened his own firm and made a go of it Él abrió su propia empresa y tuvo éxito
      My brother became a lawyer and made a go of it Mi hermano se convirtió en abogado y tuvo éxito


      make fun of - mock, ridicule

      Those children are making fun of that girl because she is wearing glasses Aquellos niños están ridiculizando a esa niña porque usa lentes
      Don't make fun of me. I'm talking seriously No te burles de mí. Estoy hablando en serio


      make it - informal, achieve

      My sister wanted to become a nurse, but she didn't make it Mi hermana se quería convertir en enfermera, pero no lo consiguió
      I wanted to work abroad and I made it Quería trabajar en el extranjero y lo conseguí


      to do one's best - Doing something as well as you can

      Mike did his best to help me, but I needed a skilled person Mike hizo su mejor esfuerzo para ayudarme, pero yo necesitaba una persona profesional
      I always do my best. In this way I don't feel guilty Siempre doy lo mejor de mí. De esta forma no me siento culpable


      to do research - looking into a topic

      My brother is doing history research at the university Mi hermano está investigando en Historia en la universidad
      I don't want to do any research. I want to teach No quiero hacer ninguna investigación. Quiero enseñar


      to do some good/harm - Acting in a helpful/bad way

      My sister is very kind-hearted. She cannot do harm Mi hermana es muy amable. No puede hacer daño
      I always do good things because it makes me feel better Siempre hago el bien porque me hace sentir mejor


      to do one's own thing - to do what one does best

      I'm very happy as a carpenter because I'm doing my own thing Estoy muy feliz de carpintero porque estoy haciendo lo mío
      Melissa is working as a secretary. She is doing her own thing Melissa está trabajando como secretaria. Está haciendo lo suyo

    •   Hollywood


      Many people between the ages of 19 and 40 want to work in the cinema, and most of those people go to Los Angeles so that they can work in Hollywood. They all have a dream to be on"the silver screen.” The film industry is quite fascinating, really. It takes thousands of different people to make a movie; from the producer to the stuntman. Each person has a specialty or special skill that they do very well. Everything has been planned to the centimeter. All of this planning starts in the script. The scriptwriter includes notes in the script to indicate what is happening. Probably two-thirds of the script is creative writing and the other one-third is directions to the actors, actresses and stuntmen.

      Each stunt that the stuntman does has to be planned exactly and measured with precision. If an error is made, an accident can happen. For example, if the stuntman has got to jump into a box from an airplane, the exact dimensions of the box is very important. If the box is only one metre by two metres, an accident is possible, and a larger box will be used, perhaps three metres by four metres. Remember that real dimensions are often not important on film. There are many special effects that directors and cameramen use to create an illusion.
