    • Making a Lovely Garden

      Gardens are relatively easy to make, but you should know a little bit about the plants that you are planting. One essential piece of information is space. Do you have enough space to grow the plant that you are planting? Oak trees, for example, have a tendency to grow slowly, so it is difficult to imagine the tree at full maturity. Willow trees need a lot of space and you should plant them away from houses and porches. Plants that have thorns should not be planted close to patios and areas where children may be playing. Do not put too many plants in your garden. A good gardening tip here is: enough is as good as a feast. Do not overplant. 

      One thing that is necessary in gardening is patience. Many people plant their garden and then watch for each new twig on a tree, or buds on a flower. Remember that when you look at the teapot, it never boils. Plants only need water, occasional fertilizer and cutting when necessary. Also, your garden will not immediately look like other gardens because the plants need time to make a home in your garden. Rome was not built in a day and your garden is no different. With a little patience, your garden will be full, green and beautiful. 

      Other than patience, diligence is very important. Weeds are the biggest enemy of the garden because they grow as quick as lightning. All it takes is a week of raining cats and dogs and you will have more weeds than flowers before you know it. Weeds also produce a lot of pollen, so if you have allergies, you will want to contain the weeds in your garden. 

      Some easy plants for children to grow are sunflowers. Children love sunflowers and they are really easy to grow. Sunflowers, however, do need sufficient space as they grow much taller and larger than other flowers. There is nothing more lovely that a photo of a child standing next to a sunflower that he or she grew. 

      So if you have never had a garden, but you are an admirer of plants and flowers, start today on your little green area. Better late than never.
