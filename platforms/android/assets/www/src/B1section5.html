    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


      A Revolutionary Documentary

      My family and I love to watch documentaries. We have watched tons of documentaries about everything from the revolution to modern writers. We are also interested in reading anything nonfiction. If a documentary is revolutionary, it really sparks my interest. I love when new writers and directors come on the scene and put together a really great documentary. In my country, there are not many good directors. Because a documentary does not have a fictional plot, the director's vision is the most important part of a documentary, although the writing is also very important.

      The other day I was in the library talking to the librarian and she recommended that I watch a new documentary that she said was revolutionary. The film was about an imaginary meeting between Fidel Castro and John F. Kennedy. The film was nonfiction, but based on imaginary circumstances, since Castro and John F. Kennedy never had this type of meeting. The film was the director's first film; although he had directed plays before. I think that the directing was pretty well-done and that the writing was excellent. The ideas expressed by the writer were really refreshing and sparkling. The cinematography was also very good, because they used a combination of old films of Castro and Kenney and new film that was created by the director.

      I think that directors that make documentaries are very admirable. It is easy to make a film if you have millions or billions of dollars; but documentaries usually count on donations to fund their movies. It is a lot more difficult for a documentary to have the money to finish the project.

      I saw in the newspaper that the director will be at a university near our house next month and I am thinking about going to meet him and possibly make a donation for any future project he has.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
