       The Crisis

      Mary is relaxing at home when she hears a knocking at the door. Knock, knock, knock. She goes to the door to see who it is.

      Mary: Susan? Hi, how are you?

      Susan: Not so good, Mary. Do you have a few minutes? I have a crisis.

      Mary: Sure. Come in. You look awful. Have you been crying?

      Susan: Yes. I am having a crisis and I need some advice.

      Mary: Tell me what happened.

      Susan: Well, it all happened when I thought I was pregnant. I knew that I had to tell Peter.

      Mary: Oh, my! Did you tell him?

      Susan: Yes, I told him. One night he came home from work. I was in the kitchen mashing potatoes and he came in to give me a kiss. I told him, "Peter, please sit down. There is something I have got to tell you.” He sat at the kitchen table and I just told him. "I think I am pregnant.”

      Mary: What did he say?

      Susan: He told me that it was probably a good time to tell me that he was having an affair with another woman. He told me that he was in love with her and that he was sorry. He said that he was thinking about leaving me.

      Mary: That is awful! What did you say?

      Susan: I told him that he has a responsibility for the baby if I am pregnant.

      Mary: Of course. What did he say?

      Susan: He suggested that I have an abortion!

      Mary: Oh, no! How do you feel about that?

      Susan: Well, of course, I do not want to have an abortion, with my strict Catholic bringing up. I completely panicked and told him to just leave right now.

      Mary: Of course. That was the right thing to do. Did he leave?

      Susan: Oh, yes, he left. And he came back, too, but just to get his clothes, then he left again.

      Mary: Are you going to get an abortion?

      Susan: Oh, I am not pregnant. Apparently I just panicked.
