    • Grammar

    •   To Be Used To Versus To Get Used Too


      to be used to - estar acostumbrado

      It isn't a modal verb, it works as a regular verb. It works the same way than "to be able to".

      subject
      		verb to be
      		used to
      		verb -ing
      		complement
      I
      		am
      		used to
      		getting up
      		early

      I am used to getting up early Estoy acostumbrado a levantarme temprano
      She was used to studying at home Ella estaba acostumbrada a estudiar en casa
      My parents will be used to surfing the net Mis padres estarán acostumbrados a surfear el Internet
      My friends have never been used to chatting Mis amigos nunca han estado acostumbrados a charlar


      to get used to - acostumbrarse

      It works in the same way as"to be used to”.

      subject
      		verb to get
      		used to
      		verb -ing
      		complement
      I
      		got
      		used to
      		studying
      		for my exams


      I got used to studying for my tests Me acostumbré a estudiar para mis exámenes
      She will get used to cooking Ella se acostumbrará a cocinar
      I got used to watching a movie every evening before going to bed Me he acostumbrado a ver una película cada noche antes de irme a dormir
      I will never get used to eating English meals Nunca me acostumbraré a comer las comidas inglesas

      Notice

      The most important difference among these verbs is the verb after them. Pay attention:
      Solía
      used to
      		infinitive
      I used to
      		work
      		very hard
      Estar acostumbrado
      be used to
      		-ing
      I am used to
      		getting up
      		early
      Acostumbrarse
      get used to
      		-ing
      She will get used to
      		cooking

      Examples:


      I used to work very hard Solía trabajar muy duro
      She is used to working hard Ella está acostumbrada a trabajar duro
      He is getting used to working hard Él se está acostumbrando a trabajar duro


      My sister used to be late Mi hermana solía llegar tarde
      Helen is used to being late Helen está acostumbrada a llegar tarde
      I'm not getting used to being late Yo no me estoy acostumbrando a llegar tarde


      Tony used to drive carefully Tonny solía conducir cuidadosamente
      My friend is used to driving carefully Mi amigo está acostumbrado a conducir cuidadosamente
      I have gotten used to driving carefully Me he acostumbrado a conducir cuidadosamente

    •   The American Real Estate Market


      Real estate in the United States has never been in worse condition than in the last two or three years. In the 1990s and between the years 2000 and 2008, Americans got used to having large, beautiful homes with swimming pools, big yards and a lot of space. The problem was that most families could not afford these homes; however, real estate agents and banks told them that a mortgage on a house that cost $160,000, for example, was possible with their salary. In many cases this was simply not true. The real estate market used to be impressive, but these days it is quite miserable.

      If you drive through many neighborhoods in the US, you will see abandoned houses that do not look attractive because nobody is taking care of them. Many neighborhoods have residents associations to monitor the maintenance of the houses in the neighborhood, the streets, parks and swimming pools. These residents associations have lost their power in the neighborhood because there are not many residents, only houses. Also, these residents' associations are funded by money paid by the residents; no residents, no money. Sure the houses have owners, but they are banks and mortgage companies. Banks and mortgage companies simply do not have the same responsibility to maintain the house as owners do.

      The cities with the most houses for sale are: Las Vegas, Nevada; Fort Myers, Florida; Miami, Florida; Riverside, California; Phoenix, Arizona. Many residents of these cities would have stayed, if they had a job; but some have left and settled into different parts of the state or another part of the country.

      Many experts say that Americans just need to get used to living in smaller, more economic houses. The fantasy of the great real estate market is finished and people need to change how they have been living in many ways. But will Americans ever be used to living in smaller houses? The American Dream has always been that every family has a nice house. Are Americans too accustomed to this lifestyle to change their habits? The next ten years will be very important for real estate in the US and you can expect to see many new trends.
