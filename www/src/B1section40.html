    • Grammar

    • Take + noun + preposition II

      take an interest in - interesarse por algo



      She took an interest in history when she had already taken her test Ella se interesó por la Historia cuando ya había hecho su examen
      I have taken an interest in tennis because I like sports Me he interesado por el tenis porque me gustan los deportes
      My mother has taken an interest in my studies and keeps asking questions about it Mi madre se ha interesado por mis estudios y me hace muchas preguntas


      take advice on - seguir el consejo de alguien en algo



      Mike took my advice on his choice of a career because he trusts me Mike siguió mi consejo en su elección de una profesión porque confía en mí
      I have taken John's advice on my travel because he is an experienced traveler He seguido el consejo de John en mi viaje porque él es un viajero experimentado
      I always took my friends' advice because they know me well Siempre sigo el consejo de mis amigos porque me conocen bien


      take responsibility for - responsabilizarse por algo



      The firm took responsibility for their illegal actions La empresa tomo responsabilidad por sus acciones ilegales
      Ruth didn't want to take responsibility for her rude behavior Ruth no quería responsabilizarse de su comportamiento grosero
      She is a mature person and she always takes responsibility for her actions Ella es una persona madura y siempre se responsabiliza de sus acciones


      take pity on someone - apiadarse de alguien



      I can't take pity on someone who is rude to everyone No puedo apiadarme por una persona que siempre es grosera con todos
      Samuel has taken pity on her because she is alone A Samuel le ha dado lástima porque ella está sola
      I took pity on the girl because she had no mother Me dio lástima la niña porque no tenía madre


      take a page out of someone's book - pasar página



      I was worrying for nothing when I should have taken a page out of my own book Me estaba preocupando por nada cuando debería haber pasado página
      I took a page out of my own book when I did that job Pasé de página cuando realicé ese trabajo
      You should take a page out of your own book when it comes to relationships Deberías pasar la página en cuanto a relaciones se refiere

      The Job Fair

      A job fair is a conference of companies that need workers, and they get together in one place in order to offer jobs to people who are looking for a job. These companies can be hotels, computer technology companies, construction companies and even international companies. The companies send their human resources managers to the job fair to advertise jobs that are open in the company. The kinds of jobs you can find at a job fair vary. The jobs can be anything from labourer, steward or security to translators or interpreters and computer technicians. You can basically go shopping for any job that you take an interest in. For example, if you have taken an interest in the restaurant industry, but you do not have any experience, you can go to the job fair and try to get a job as a steward in order to start your restaurant career. 

      If you are good at taking responsibility for security, a security guard job may be good for you, because they ask for a lot of responsibility for that job. If you have taken an interest in selling things, you can talk to a company that is looking for salesmen. Sometimes you need experience for the job and sometimes no experience is necessary. 

      If you are not sure what kind of job is best for you and your experience, there are frequently people at the job fair that can help you with some advice. These are people with a lot of experience and you can trust their advice. You should take their advice on a career choice if you are not sure what your best option is. Maybe you are a really good artist; however, you never finished university, but they might take pity on you and consider you for a job as a designer. 

      Remember that companies are there looking for fresh workers and they sometimes have different standards at job fairs. Their top priority is to find talent. What is your special talent? Think about your special talents. Are you good with languages? Do you speak more than one language? Did you study English in university? Consider a job as an interpreter. Some jobs ask you to take a course for certification or to complete your studies. The companies normally pay for these courses, but they may not pay you for your time to take the course. Do not be afraid to ask questions. If the job requires this kind of training, be sure to ask about pay during the training course. A job that you like should be your number one priority. When people have jobs that they like, they are happier and more satisfied with life.
