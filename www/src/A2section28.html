    • Grammar

    •  Phrasal Verbs: bring up, look after, break down, wake up

      bring up - educar


      I like bringing up my children. I love being a mom Me gusta criar a mis hijos. Amo ser mamá
      My mom brought up five children, so she knows a lot about the matter Mi madre educó cinco niños, así es que ella sabe mucho del tema
      I am bringing up my son the way I like Estoy educando a mi hijo de la manera que me gusta

      look after - cuidar


      I cannot go to the movie theater tonight; I am looking after my little cousin No puedo ir al cine esta noche; estoy cuidando a mi primo pequeño
      I look after my brother every Sunday while mom works in the hospital Cuido a mi hermano todos los domingos mientras mamá trabaja en el hospital
      I love looking after little children, I am a babysitter Me encanta cuidar niños pequeños, soy niñera

      break down - romper a llorar, tener un ataque nervioso


      You shout at me so much that I am having a nervous breakdown Me gritas tanto que estoy teniendo un ataque nervioso
      Mike worked day and night on the project. When he finished, he had a breakdown Mike trabajó día y noche en el proyecto. Cuando terminó, tuvo un ataque nervioso
      Mary broke down when her husband said he wanted the divorce Mary rompió a llorar cuando su marido le dijo que quería el divorcio

      wake up - despertarse


      She doesn't wake up early on the weekends Ella no se levanta temprano el fin de semana
      I don't like to wake up because there is noise at home No me gusta despertarme porque hay ruido en casa
      I wake up very early, but I get up a couple of hours later Me despierto muy temprano, pero me levanto un par de horas más tarde

       The Babysitter


      Ramona is a babysitter and that means she looks after other people's children. The children are usually good kids because their parents bring them up correctly. In fact, Ramona is normally happy to look after kids. Kids love her and she loves them. One day a neighbor asked her to watch the kids for the afternoon. Ramona needed some money, so she agreed. She had never looked after these kids before. 

      The afternoon started great. The kids were playing, so Ramona decided to make them a nice lunch. She was looking up a recipe for spaghetti in a cookbook, while the kids were in the living room. She was distracted looking for the ingredients and did not notice that the kids were really quiet. When she went into the living room she saw a disaster. The kids were playing with rubber cement and had put some on their eyelashes with their fingers and now they cannot open their eyes! Ramona ran to the kitchen for some warm water to wash them up. When she was coming back, she put her foot in the water and fell. Oh, what a pain she felt. She looked down and thought that she had broken her ankle. The kids were crying now and Ramona was on the floor in pain. The kids cannot open their eyes, so they do not know that she is hurt. She stands up and walks across the room. The ankle is okay, not broken. The kids are crying and crying now because they cannot open their eyes and are pulling their eyelids to take off the cement. Ramona thought,"I am going to have a nervous breakdown if they don't stop crying!” She cleaned the cement from their eyelids and eyelashes, calmed down, and made lunch. By the time their mother had come home, everything was normal again.
