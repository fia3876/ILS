    • Grammar

    •   Gradación de los Adjetivos I : La comparación

      Para graduar los adjetivos usamos tres tipos de comparación:


        1 - Comparación de Inferioridad:

      Debemos usar la estructura: less + adjetivo + than que traducimos a español como menos +adjetivo + que, así podemos decir:



      Less heavy than Menos pesado que

      Less tall than Menos alto que

      Lees dangerous than Menos peligroso que

      Bob is less punctual than you Bob es menos puntual que tú

      Your sister is less tall than you Tu hermana es menos alta que tú

      This job is less interesting than the other one Este trabajo es menos interesante que el otro

        2 - Comparación de Igualdad:

      Debemos usar la estructura as + adjetivo + as para expresar la igualdad entre dos cosas. La traducimos a español como tan + adjetivo + como, decimos de esta manera:





      The chair is as expensive as the table La silla es tan cara como la mesa

      To watch TV is as boring as going to school Ver la Televisión es tan aburrido como ir al colegio


      Usamos la estructura not as + adjetivo + as para expresar la diferencia entre dos cosas. La traducimos a español como " no tan + adjetivo + como”, así podemos decir:



      She´s not as strong as me Ella no es tan fuerte como yo

      You are not as punctual as him Tú no eres tan puntual como él

      Mary is not as clever as Jonh Mary no es tan inteligente como John


      Podemos usar la misma estructura con adverbios en lugar de adjetivos para expresar la igualdad o la diferencia en la manera de hacer las cosas: (not) as + adverbio + as traducido a español de la misma forma (no) tan + adverbio + como



      He runs as quickly as her Él corre tan rápido como ella

      Mike cooks as well Peter Mike cocina tan bien como Peter


      También usamos esta estructura con nombres contables e incontables para hablar de cantidades iguales o diferentes de cosas o personas. Para ello utilizamos las siguientes estructuras:

      - Para nombres contables:

      not as many + nombre contable plural + as traducido como"no tantos, -as + nombre contable plural + como”:



      He doesn´t have as many friends as you Él no tiene tantos amigos como tú

      Jane doesn´t have as many books as Ruth Jane no tiene tantos libros como Ruth


      - Para nombres incontables:

      not as much + nombre incontable + as traducido como"no tanto + nombre incontable + como” :



      Paris has as much traffic as Madrid París tiene tanto tráfico como Madrid

      Jack doesn´t do as much work as me Jack no hace tanto trabajo como yo




      Pronombres:

      Podemos usar los pronombres de sujeto pero en este caso deben ir seguidos del verbo de la oración:



      Jane is as clever as he is Jane es tan inteligente como él


      También podemos usar los pronombres de objeto, en cuyo caso podrían ir solos ya que la partícula "as” se entiende como una preposición:



      Peter is as tall as her Peter es tan alto como ella

      I have the same car as my sister Yo tengo el mismo coche que mi hermana

        Nota

      Recuerda que para comprar cosas siempre usamos as

    •   What Will I Be?

      Anne and her mother are in the kitchen making breakfast. The girl is having a terrible time deciding which courses to choose for school. Part of the problem is that she can't decide what she wants to be when she grows up.

      She's thinking about her love for books and reading. But work in a book store or library isn't as exciting as other jobs to her. So she asks her Mom what she thinks. Her mom suggests travel. It's more exciting to see different parts of the world. She can do this as a journalist, her mom suggests; or maybe a fashion designer. Of course, a journalist's job isn't as easy as a librarian's job.

      Anne is finishing her eggs and toast and decides to think about it later. She has to get ready for school. She packs her gym bag and gets ready for today's exercise class. Her teacher this year isn't as good as the teacher last year.

      She has to find her sneakers. She doesn't want to be as late as she was yesterday. She looks around and finds her sneakers under her bed.

      Her books aren't as heavy as usual and she walks to the bus stop with her book bag, her gym bag, and her lunch. The weather is as fine as it was yesterday.

    • Lee el texto y decide si las frases son verdaderas (T) o falsas (F).
