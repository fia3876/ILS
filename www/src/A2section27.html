    • Grammar

    •  Anymore, any longer, no longer

      not ___ anymore

      Usamos "not ___ anymore" cuando queremos decir que una situación ha llegado a su fin y no se repitirá más. 

      Lo colocamos a final de frase.



      I'm not living in London anymore. I live in Oxford now Ya no vivo en Londres. Vivo en Oxford
      George is not working as a plumber anymore. He is studying law now George ya no trabaja de plomero. Está estudiando derecho ahora
      Paul is not my boyfriend anymore. We broke up last week Paul ya no es mi novio. Rompimos la semana pasada
      I'm not studying at the university anymore. I finished last month Yo no estoy estudiando en la universidad. Terminé el mes pasado


      not ___ any longer

      Usamos "not ___ any longer" con el mismo sentido y en la misma posición:

      Susan is not travelling any longer. She came back home last weekend Susan ya no está viajando. Volvió a casa el fin de semana pasado
      You are not joking about your sister any longer. Go to your room Tú ya no bromearás más sobre tu hermana. Vete a tu habitación
      My parents are not looking for a house any longer. They found a really nice one yesterday Mis padres ya no están buscando casa. Ellos encontraron una bastante agradable


      no longer 
      También podemos usar con este mismo sentido la expresión "no longer". En este caso el verbo irá en afirmativo. Su posición es igual que la de los adverbios de frecuencia, es decir, trás el sujeto de la oración y delante del verbo:

      John no longer lives here John no vive aquí ya


      Trás el sujeto y detrás del verbo to be:

      I am no longer a doctor Yo ya no soy médico


      Ejemplos de uso


      He is no longer single, he got married last Sunday Él ya no está soltero, él se casó el domingo pasado
      I don't study English anymore, I speak quite fluently now Ya no estudio inglés, hablo con bastante fluidez ahora
      They are no longer a couple. They broke up last month Ellos ya no son pareja. Ellos rompieron el mes pasado
      They don't work in the restaurant any longer. They left the city Ellos ya no trabajan en el restaurante. Ellos se fueron de la ciudad
      I'm not sharing an apartment anymore. I bought a beautiful house Ya no comparto departamento. Me compré una bonita casa
      Mike is not taking the train anymore. He has a car now Mike ya no toma el tren. Él tiene un coche ahora
      Ruth no longer works as an actress. She is fed up with the job Ruth no trabaja ya como actriz. Ella está harta del trabajo



      Jule doesn't smoke anymore. The doctor said he cannot Jule ya no fuma. El médico dijo que no podía
      I don't worry about you anymore. You are a liar Ya no me preocupo por ti. Eres un mentiroso
      Edward is not reading any longer. He is tired Edward ya no lee más. Él está cansado
      I no longer meet Frank. He is not my friend now Yo ya no quedo con Frank. Él no es mi amigo ahora
      George no longer walks. He had a serious accident George ya no camina. Él tuvo un accidente grave
      My friends are not going to that pub anymore. They are tired of it Mis amigos ya no van a ese bar. Están cansados de él

    •  She Just Lost it!

      Elvira Huckleberry has just lost 300 pounds on a new diet plan that everybody is talking about. All of the radio and TV shows want to interview her to ask her what her secret is. This is an interview on a radio show. 

      Radio: Elvira, you have lost 300 pounds. How long did it take you to lose it all? 

      Elvira: Well, you cannot lose that much weight quickly, so it did take a while, more than a year. 

      Radio: And how did you lose this amazing weight? 

      Elvira: Frankly, healthy lifestyle. I always ate things that I liked: chips, ice cream, cakes. I do not eat those things anymore. Now I eat carrots, broccoli, steamed fish and chicken. No more chips with the fish! 

      Radio: So you simply changed your diet? 

      Elvira: Oh, no. There are many changes necessary to have a healthy lifestyle. Before I was watching a lot of TV and I never walked anywhere. I always drove or took a taxi. I no longer drive, but instead I walk. I must admit that I no longer feel so tired! 

      Radio: Are there any secrets you can tell the people listening about how to make a change in your lifestyle. 

      Elvira: I just decided one day that I did not want to be fat anymore. You just have to make up your mind: No more! I was just fed up with not having a boyfriend. 

      Radio: Well, thank you, Elvira. I understand that you are single? 

      Elvira: Not anymore. I got married last month. 

      Radio: Congratulations!
