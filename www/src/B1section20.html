    • Grammar

    •   False Friends


      billion - mil millones

      trillion - billón


      Last year McDonalds sold a billion hamburgers El año pasado McDonalds vendió mil millones de hamburguesas
      That old man is very wealthy. His company earns over a billion euros per year Ese hombre mayor es muy rico. Su compañía gana más de mil millones de euros al año


      camp - campamento

      countryside - campo


      The kids are spending one month in a camp next summer Los niños van a pasar un mes en un campamento el verano que viene
      I love walking in the field when I'm in the countryside Me encanta caminar por el campo cuando estoy en el campo


      college - facultad

      school - colegio

      high school - instituto

      My college is famous worldwide. There are many foreign students Mi facultad es famosa por todo el mundo. Hay muchos estudiantes extranjeros
      My younger sister is at school. She is eleven years old Mi hermana pequeña está en el colegio. Tiene 11 años
      The king's rules made everyone in the kingdom poor Las reglas del rey hicieron al reino pobre


      content - satisfecho

      happy - contento


      The coach was really content with the result of the match El entrenador estaba realmente satisfecho con el resultado del partido
      I'm always happy on weekends Siempre estoy contento los fines de semana


      exigence - necesidad

      demand - exigencia


      Having enough food was an exigency difficult to fulfill at the beginning of the twentieth century Tener suficiente comida era una necesidad difícil de cubrir a principios del siglo veinte
      I'm not going to pay attention to his demands No voy a prestarle atención a sus exigencias


      familiar - familiar adj.

      relative - relativo noun

      Do I know you? Your face is very familiar to me ¿Te conozco? Tú cara me es muy familiar
      This is Francis. She's my relative. She's my cousin's daughter  Esta es Francis. Es familiar mía. Es la hija de mi primo


      notice - anuncio

      news - noticia

      advertisement/ad - anuncio


      Have you watched the news on TV? Something is happening in Italy ¿Has visto las noticias en la tele? Algo está ocurriendo en Italia
      Don't you see the notice? You can read: Non-smoking Area ¿No ves el anuncio? Puedes leer: Zona de No fumadores
      We should talk it over before making a decision Deberíamos discutirlo antes de tomar una decisión
      Have you seen this ad? You can get a new cell for free ¿Has visto este anuncio? Puedes conseguir un nuevo teléfono gratis


      fabric - tejido

      factory - fábrica


      I love your sweater. It's very soft. Which fabric is it made of? Me encanta tu jersey. Es muy suave. ¿De qué tejido está hecho?
      My mom worked in a shoe factory, but she isn't working now Mi madre trabajaba en una fábrica de zapatos, pero ella no está trabajando ahora


      marmalade - mermelada de cítricos

      jam - mermelada

      I don't really like orange marmalade. It tastes weird Realmente no me gusta la mermelada de naranja. Sabe raro
      I like having a toast with jam and butter in the morning Me gusta tomarme una tostada con mermelada y mantequilla por la mañana

    •   Cooperative Farming

      Billions of people worldwide are enjoying fresh food from the farm at cheaper prices than the supermarket. The demand for fresher and cheaper vegetables that came directly from the fields of small farms has created a market based on exigency. Farmers bring their fresh vegetables and fruits to cooperative markets where people can find certain kinds of vegetables that are not familiar to the common person, like purple carrots and baby endives. Supermarkets make trillions of dollars from selling vegetables and fruits from commercial farms; and these commercial farms grow billions of vegetables and fruits to sell in supermarkets; however, commercial farms are very similar to factories.

      People that live in the city want the fresh country food on their tables and are happy to go to the farmer's markets on the weekends with their family. Cooperative markets are advertised in the newspapers by putting a notice that says how many farms will be participating and where and when the market will take place.

      A news station in California has also helped to advertise the farmers markets. The farmers market in San Francisco, California, is open three days a week--Tuesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays. On Tuesdays and Thursdays, the smaller markets occupy the front of the building along the Embarcadero; on Saturdays, there is a larger market in front of the Ferry Building in the plaza overlooking the Bay. The markets offer fruits, vegetables, herbs, flowers, meats and eggs from small regional farmers and ranchers, many of whom are certified organic. Other artesian products such as breads, cheeses and jams are also offered. The Thursday market has artisan street food like pizza, grilled meats, sandwiches, and tacos, while the Saturday market also includes local restaurants serving a variety of hot, delicious meals.
