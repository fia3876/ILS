    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


      Living in England for just a few weeks

      Sally is an American, who has been living in England for just a few weeks. She has managed to get a job at a British hotel as a receptionist. She has worked as a receptionist before, but in the States. She knows that British vocabulary is different from American vocabulary and that she will have to learn the new vocabulary in order to be a good receptionist. Her manager has agreed to help her learn the British vocabulary.

      Manager: The first thing you should know is that we British refer to making a phone call as"ring” or "ring up.” You can give somebody a ring, it is similar to calling somebody.

      Sally: Oh, really? We say that the phone rings, so that is logical to me.

      Manager: I believe that in the States they say"give a buzz.” Is that right?

      Sally: Well,"give me a buzz” is like saying a brief conversation. It is short. A buzz.

      Manager: Now, if they want to know a phone number, it is a directory enquiry.

      Sally: Oh, like calling information to get the phone number. Okay. I understand.

      Manager: Sometimes they want to reverse the charges so that they do not have to pay when they check out.

      Sally: So that is something like a collect call. Interesting.

      Manager: That is right. A collect call. Another type of call is a personal call.

      Sally: I thought all calls were personal.

      Manager: In this situation, it means that you call to speak to only person; and if that person is not there, you do not pay for the call.

      Sally: Oh, a person-to-person call is what we call it in the States.

      Manager: Sometimes they want you to put them through to another room in the hotel.

      Sally: Put them through? Do you mean connect them?

      Manager: Exactly. Sometimes they need assistance with trunk calls.

      Sally: Trunk calls?

      Manager: Yes, calls outside the city or the area.

      Sally: Long distance calls. Certainly.

      Manager: You may need to help them with the dialing codes, and there is a list of dialing codes here of each city.

      Sally: Oh, wow, like area codes.

      Manager: Are there any questions?

      Sally: I don't think so. That will help me a lot.


      Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
