    • Will, Would and Shall

      You already know the verbs"will” and "would”. However, we are studying them deeper in this unit.


      Will and Would

      will - we use it to express the future.

      would- we use it as a conditional, remember that the conditional expresses a theory.

      However, these are not their only uses and translations. We can also translate these verbs as

      querer when we want to ask something in a formal way or make an invitation:




      Will you have a cup of tea? ¿Te apetece un té?
      Would you give me an application form, please? ¿Querría darme una solicitud, por favor?
      Will you come to the party? ¿Quieres venir a la fiesta?
      Would you share our meal? ¿Querrías comer con nosotros?
      Will you hand me a paper, please? ¿Quiere pasarme un periódico, por favor?
      Would you come to the movie theater? ¿Querrías venir al cine?


      Shall

      It is an old form for"will”. Most of its forms disappeared during the language evolution and

      nowadays it's not very common to use it. It works exactly as "will" does:


      I shall go with you Iré contigo
      I shall not go with you No iré contigo
      Shall I go with you? ¿Iré contigo?

      Notice

      The negative form of shall is: shan't.


      I shan't help you Yo no te ayudaré

      Its most typical use is in the first or third person. We use it to make suggestions or offerings

      and to ask for instructions and permission.



      I shall help you Te ayudaré
      Shall I go with him? ¿Debo ir con él?
      I shall try it Yo lo intentaría


      Examples:


      I shall open the window Abriré la ventana
      Will you come to the concert? ¿Te quieres venir al concierto?
      Shall I help you with dinner? ¿Te ayudo con la cena?
      Would you join the gym? ¿Te querrías apuntar al gimnasio?
      Shall I walk her home? ¿La acompaño a casa?
      Will you help me with my math test? ¿Quieres ayudarme con mi examen de Matemáticas?
      Shall I turn on the heating? ¿Enciendo la calefacción?
      Will you have a milkshake? ¿Quieres un batido?

       A Romantic Evening

      Karla and Michael are having a lovely romantic evening in a first-class restaurant. It is a special occasion, their anniversary. The restaurant they chose has the best service in the city and they are looking forward to relaxing and spending time together.

      Karla: Would you like to sit by the windows? The view is quite lovely and I don't think that it is too cold over there

      Michael: I will sit wherever you want me to, darling.

      Karla: You are so romantic tonight, Michael.

      Michael: I love you so much, Karla. I am just glad to be with you this evening.

      Karla: That is so nice, honey. I love you, too.

      Waiter: Would you like to see the wine menu?

      Michael: Yes, we will order some wine. What about a red wine, Karla?

      Karla: A red wine sounds fine. What about an appetizer? Would you like an appetizer?

      Michael: Yes, I think that the escargot will be nice. What do you think?

      Karla: That sounds great. And I will have the fish and shrimp for a main course.

      Michael: I will have the beef tenderloin and jacket potato.

      Waiter: All right. I will be right back with your bottle of wine. Would you like any sparkling water?

      Karla: Not for me.

      Michael: I believe I will have some. Thank you.

      Karla: Oh, I love this song! It is one of my favorites. Shall we dance?

      Michael: Yes, we shall.

      Karla: What a wonderful evening, Michael, I love you so much.

      Michael: That's good, Karla, because I have something to ask you. We have been going out together for 3 years.

      Karla: That's right.

      Michael: Karla, I was thinking the other day. There is no other woman in the world who would make me happy; only you. If I asked you, would you marry me?

      Karla: Are you asking me now?

      Michael: Yes, I guess I am. Karla, will you marry me?

      Karla: Yes, I will marry you, Michael. When shall we marry?

      Michael: Well, I was thinking that we shall marry tomorrow. What do you say?

      Karla: Yes, I will.
