      despite - a pesar de



      They decided to go ahead with the plan despite their requests to stop Ellos decidieron seguir adelante con el proyecto a pesar de sus solicitudes de parar
      He is a good cook despite his bad manners Él es un buen cocinero a pesar de sus malos modales
      He loves her despite her changing mood Él la ama a pesar de su humor cambiante
      Susan didn't help him despite her kindness Susan no le ayudó a pesar de su amabilidad
      I went to work despite my illness Fui a trabajar a pesar de mi enfermedad


      unlike- diferente a, a diferencia de, poco característico



      It's unlike him to be late Es poco característico de él llegar tarde
      Their worldwide success is unlike any other Su éxito por todo el mundo es muy diferente a cualquier otro


      but for - a no ser por, excepto



      We would have arrived on time but for Frank. He got lost trying to find your house Nosotros habríamos llegado a tiempo de no ser por Frank. Él se perdió intentando encontrar tu casa
      I wouldn't have passed that test but for Julie. She explained everything to me Yo no habría aprobado ese examen a no ser por July. Ella me lo explicó todo
      I wouldn't have known the answers but for my friend. He told me what to write Yo no habría sabido las respuestas a no ser por mi amigo. Él me dijo que escribir


      close to - cerca de



      Your bag is close to the computer. Can you find it? Tu bolso está cerca del ordenador. ¿Lo encuentras?
      I finish classes around six. We can meet close to half past six if you want Termino las clases sobre las seis. Podemos quedar cerca de las seis y media si quieres
      My house is close to the park, so I usually walk there in the afternoon Mi casa está cerca del parque, así que normalmente camino allí por la tarde
      I am close to making a decision, but I need to meditate about it a bit longer Estoy cerca de tomar una decisión, pero necesito meditarlo un poco más
      Your glasses are close to the dictionary, don't you see them? Tus gafas están cerca del diccionario, ¿No las ves?

    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


       Pilot's Lessons

      Frank: Hey, Ted, how was your weekend?

      Ted: Actually, it was great! You know, I have been taking flying lessons and I am really close to getting my pilot's license.

      Frank: Really? I did not realize that you were learning to fly.

      Ted: Yeah. Despite the fact that I have not had much time to devote to the lessons, I was finally able to finish almost all the lessons necessary to take the exam to be a pilot.

      Frank: That is great! So what is it like to be a pilot? Is it exciting to fly a plane?

      Ted: Oh, yes. When you learn to fly a plane, it is unlike any other experience you will have.

      Frank: I would love to do it, but it's expensive, isn't it?

      Ted: It is expensive. Luckily for me, the plane that I have been using is my father-in-law's. He has been letting me use it. But for his help, I would not have been able to take the lessons. And his plane is not just any plane, it is really nice.

      Frank: So is it difficult?

      Ted: Not at all. You just need to practice. I think that the most difficult part is to learn to land the plane. That takes some practice.

      Frank: So does your wife go up in the airplane with you?

      Ted: Not yet. She did not want me to be a pilot at first. She was very scared and would not go to the airport with me. My father-in-law talked to her and explained that is it not very likely that a pilot will have an accident or get lost, especially with the new GPS devices that most planes come with these days. Now she understands that it is not likely that a pilot will have an accident.

      Frank: She must be a lovely girl.

      Ted: She is. When I get my pilot's license, she will be my first passenger. Would you like to go up with me some day?

      Frank: I would love to be one of your passengers. Just tell me when you are close to getting your license and I will go with you.

      Ted: Great!

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
