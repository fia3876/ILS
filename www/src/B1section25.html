    • Should versus Ought to

      should - debería 

      ought to - debería 

      They are the conditional form of the verb "must". They work as every modal verb does:


      You should go home Deberías irte a casa
      You shouldn't go home No deberías irte a casa
      Should you go home? ¿Deberías irte a casa?
      He should look for a job Él debería buscar un trabajo
      He shouldn't look for a job Él no debería buscar un trabajo
      Should he look for a job? ¿Debería él buscar un trabajo?
      I ought to find a new house Yo debería encontrar una nueva casa
      I oughtn't to find a new house Yo no debería encontrar una nueva casa
      Ought I to find a new house? ¿Debería encontrar una nueva casa?
      Mike ought to study harder Mike debería estudiar más duramente
      Mike oughtn't to study harder Mike no debería estudiar más duramente
      Ought Mike to study harder? ¿Debería Mike estudiar más duramente?


      Notice

      As you can see they both work in the same way and have the same meaning. However, "should" is more common than "ought to".


      Must, Should and Ought To:
      "Must" has the strongest meaning. "Should" and "ought to" are weaker. In other words, when you give an advice, if you use the word "should" it won't be as strong as "must".


      You must study harder Debes estudiar más duramente
      You should study harder Deberías estudiar más duramente



      Examples:




      He should get up earlier Él debería levantarse más temprano
      I ought to go to bed Yo debería irme a la cama
      My sister should be careful Mi hermana debería tener cuidado
      My friend oughtn't to worry Mi amigo no debería preocuparse

    • Graduation Day

      It is graduation day at university and one of the people speaking at the graduation is Celia Downs, a graduate of the university who has been a quite successful businesswoman. Ms. Downs is something of a celebrity and the students are excited to hear what advice she can give them to start their own successful business careers. 

      Ms. Downs: Hello everybody. I speak to you today as an ex-student and graduate of this wonderful university. I must thank Professor Rogers for inviting me to come here to talk to you; and I must tell you that you are very fortunate to graduate from such an excellent school. Today is the first day of the rest of your career and you ought to be very excited about that. You ought to be glad that you have graduated and that you have bright and beautiful futures to look forward to. 

      I should start with my story. I graduated from this university 25 years ago and I am very happy to be able to see your fresh, young faces here today. I have been very fortunate and I should thank my professors for all of my success. If not for them, I may not be as successful today as I am. 

      One of the most important lessons that I have learned while studying here at this school is that nothing comes easy in life. You ought to pay attention to what is most important to you. I had to work many long hours to get what I wanted in life. It is not easy to obtain success and you should be prepared to put a lot of effort into getting what you want out of life. You should decide what your objective is. You must do it with all of your effort. You should not take the easy road because it will not take you anywhere. You ought to be ready to devote your life to your career and you ought to continue like that until you see results. So my advice to you here today, is: You must depend on you. You ought to give it all of your effort and then you should have the kind of success that I have enjoyed. And you should never take the easy road.

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
