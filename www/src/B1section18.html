    • Grammar


    • Inversión del Sujeto

      You know the normal sentence order in English is Subject + Verb + Complement. However, there are some expressions that we can place at the beginning of the sentence. When we place these expressions at the beginning of the sentence, we change the Subject +Verb order into Verb + Subject.


      hardly - apenas


      Hardly had you phoned when she came in

      Hardly had you phoned when she came in Apenas habías llamado cuando entró ella
      Hardly was he leaving when I arrived Apenas se estaba yendo él cuando yo llegué
      Hardly is he trying when you shout to him Apenas lo está intentando cuando le gritas


      no sooner ___ than - tan pronto como, no más que


      No sooner had I finished work than you phoned me

      No sooner had I finished work than you phoned me No había hecho más que terminar el trabajo cuando llamaste
      No sooner had he gone than we needed his help Tan pronto como se hubo ido, necesitamos su ayuda
      No sooner had he started working than his sister was bothering him Tan pronto como él estaba trabajando su hermana lo estaba molestando

      Simple Present or Simple Past

      When the verbal tense is a Simple Present or Simple Past, we place the auxiliary verb before the subject:

      No sooner had he called than you asked me for him Tan pronto como llamó, preguntaste por él
      Hardly do you ask for the menu when the waiter is here Apenas preguntas por el menú cuando el mesero está aquí


      in vain - en vano


      In vain do I try again and again

      In vain do I try again and again En vano lo intento una y otra vez
      In vain did we study so much En vano estudiamos tanto
      In vain has he come today En vano ha venido él hoy
      In vain is Miriam helping that girl En vano está Miriam ayudando a esa chica


      only - solamente


      Only with some advice will he be able to do this task

      Only with some advice will he be able to do this task Solamente con algo de consejo será capaz él de hacer esta tarea
      Only with your effort can you improve your skills Solamente con esfuerzo puedes mejorar tus habilidades
      Only with my friends will I go to that party Solamente con mis amigos iré a esa fiesta
      Only working hard will he get good results Solamente trabajando duro conseguirá buenos resultados


      under - bajo


      Under no circumstances will I try that

      Under no circumstances will I try that Bajo ninguna circunstancia probaré eso
      Under no pressure does he stop Bajo ninguna presión él se detiene
      Under no excuse can you stay in today Bajo ninguna excusa puedes quedarte en casa hoy
      Under no problem must you worry Bajo ningún problema debes preocuparte

    • Reading

    • Reading: Read the text carefully. It may appear in the text words that have not yet been studied, for these cases we recommend using a dictionary. After reading the text several times, answer the following questions.


      Learning to Ride a Motorcycle

      I have always wanted to learn to ride a motorcycle. When I see those guys riding down the road on that big machine, I imagine that it is me. I would love to know what it is like to be on a machine, going down the road, with the wind blowing my hair, the sun shining down on me. It must be marvelous!

      One of my cousins has a motorcycle and I have been telling him that I have got to ride it. He asserted that it is easy and I believed him. So one weekend, I decided to ring up my cousin and convince him to let me try

      He explained how everything on the bike worked. I was ready to go flying down the road on my machine! I was really looking forward to living my fantasy.

      No sooner had I started my travel, when I had problems. I was having a problem balancing the bike. Only with a lot of effort could I keep the balance. Because the bike was not balanced, my calf touched the hot engine and burned me. Hardly had I felt the hot motor, when the bike started to go out of control. In vain was I trying to control the bike, but I crashed into a post on the side of the road. I went flying and hurt my groin. Only after resting for a few minutes, did I stand up to check the rest of my body. The engine burned my pants and I could see that it took some flesh off of my calf. Only after about an hour, did I notice that I had injured my knuckle, too. I was in such pain!

      Under no circumstance will I ever try that again. I will leave those magnificent machines to the other guys and I will just admire them as they go down the road, the wind blowing in their hair

    • Read the text and decide if the sentences are true (T) or false (F).
