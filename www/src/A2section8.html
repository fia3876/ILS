    • Grammar

    •  Bring and Take: Bring

    • Bring and take 

      Estos dos verbos se podrían llamar complementarios en cuanto a su significado, bring significa "traer” y take en su sentido más literal significa "llevar”, aunque comprobarás más adelante que éste es uno de sus muchos significados:


      Can you bring a spoon, please? ¿Puedes traer una cuchara, por favor?
      Take an umbrella, it's raining Lleva un paraguas, está lloviendo
      Bring me some Coke when you go to the supermarket Tráeme Coca Cola cuando vayas al supermercado
      Don't worry! I'll take you to the train station ¡No te preocupes! Te llevaré a la estación de tren

      Existen algunas expresiones hechas con estos verbos con las que es interesante que te familiarices, las estudiaremos por separado:

      bring into force - hacer ley



      They will bring the new rules into force by August Ellos harán ley las nuevas reglas en Agosto
      The government brought the regulation into force last year El gobierno hizo el reglamento legal el año pasado

      bring to light - revelar

      He didn't want them to bring his secret to light Él no quería que ellos revelaran su secreto
      Helen brought that sad story of him to light Helen reveló esta historia triste de él

      bring to a head - llegar al punto en que es necesario hacer cambios

      They brought the matter to a head when the thief robbed them Ellos llevaron el asunto a un punto crítico cuando el ladrón les robó
      Mark brought his marriage to a head because he didn't bear it anymore Mark llevó su matrimonio a un punto crítico porque él no lo soportaba más

      bring into the open - hacer público


      They brought their engagement into the open at Christmas dinner Ellos hicieron público su compromiso en la cena de Navidad

      Oscar brought all his secrets into the open because he was drunk Oscar hizo públicos todos sus secretos porque él estaba borracho

      bring to perfection - llevar a la perfección

      He brought his career to perfection when he finished that job Él llevó su carrera a la perfección cuando terminó ese trabajo
      Tom brought his life to perfection when he got married Tom llevó su vida a la perfección cuando se casó

      bring out the best - ser lo mejor de uno mismo


      Ruth and Kathy are very good friends. They bring out the best of each other Ruth and Kathy son muy buenas amigas. Ellas sacan lo mejor la una de la otra
      Jude is my sister-in-law. I like her because she brings out the best in my brother Jude es mi cuñada. Me gusta porque saca lo mejor de mi hermano

      bring home the bacon - ganarse el pan


      He took that job in the restaurant because he wanted to bring home the bacon Él tomó ese trabajo en el restaurante porque él quería llevar el pan a casa
      Mike is a hard-working man, they are a large family and he always brings home the bacon Mike es un hombre muy trabajador, ellos son una familia grande y él siempre se gana el pan

    •  A Movie Star's Life


      My name is Lupita Hayward. I guess you can say that I am a famous person, but actually, I am just an actress. Actresses are real people too, and they have real lives, just like you and me. I think the worst part about being an actress is the newspaper and television press. They just love to bring every little thing about me into the open. If I go to an ice cream shop, they have to bring it to light. How interesting is that? I don't think it is interesting at all, but the paparazzi certainly do! There actually need to be some laws about this kind of behavior by the press. I think some rules about what the paparazzi can and cannot do are important to protect the private lives of famous people.

      The paparazzi were not always interested in me. I guess I was boring or maybe not famous enough, but little by little they were interested in what I was doing. I started to be apprehensive about the situation. Finally one day, something happened that brought it all to a head. I had got engaged to my current husband. I announced the engagement in the press, but that did not satisfy the paparazzi! I saw them every place that I went! It was unbelievable how they acted like little children. At first, I did not care, but eventually I was scared of them and the situation that they were causing. Then after my marriage to my husband and the birth of my first child, they really became more interested in my private life. I became more and more angry and upset with the press and how they treated me; so, one day I put some ice cream on the lens of one of the cameramen. Oh, he was angry! He said I had ruined his camera. I guess the paparazzi do not bring out the best in me!

      I certainly hope that one day the government will decide that some rules are really necessary and bring into force some laws to protect my private life.
