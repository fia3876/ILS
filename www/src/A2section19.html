      Ejemplos de uso:



      He wanted that job, but they didn't give it to him because he is illiterate Él quería ese trabajo, pero ellos no se lo dieron porque es analfabeta
      George smoked in there; but it was a nonsmoking room, so the policeman was angry George fumó allí; pero era una habitación para no fumadores, así es que la policía se enfadó
      I only drink non alcoholic beer, it's better for my pregnancy Yo sólo bebo cerveza sin alcohol, es mejor para mi embarazo
      They didn't want to play because they said it was an illogical game Ellos no querían jugar porque decían que era un juego ilógico
      Her attendance at school is quite irregular. She doesn't go to class sometimes Su asistencia en la escuela es muy irregular. Ella no va a clases en ocasiones
      I don't like decaffeinated coffee. I prefer regular coffee No me gusta el café descafeinado. Prefiero uno normal


      It's illegal to drive faster than fifty kilometers per hour into town Es ilegal conducir más rápido de 50 kilómetros por hora en poblado
      Your car isn't working and it's irreparable Su coche no funciona y es irreparable
      This is a nonsmoking bar. Go outside to smoke, please Este es un bar de no fumadores. Vaya afuera a fumar, por favor
      He is quite an irresponsible person, don't trust him Él es una persona bastante irresponsable, no confíes en él

    • Reading: Lee el texto detenidamente. Puede que en el texto figuren palabras que todavía no se han estudiado, para estos casos aconsejamos el uso de un diccionario. Después de leer el texto varias veces, responde las preguntas que figuran del mismo.


        Who goes to jail?


      Recent research shows that the types of people who end up in jail have a tendency to be the same. Irresponsibility is high on the list of characteristics that these people have, followed by illiterate. There is a high percentage of people in jail who can neither read nor write. It appears that there is a connection between illiteracy and delinquent behavior. Some people react aggressively, doing illegal things, when they feel devalued as a human being. 

      Another sector of this group is the alcoholics. In the research, psychologists discovered that people who are alcoholics are not as rational as people who do not drink alcohol. This irrationality creates criminals who will end up in jail. 

      Religion plays a big part also; nonbelievers or those who have no religious beliefs are at risk to become criminals. Nonconformists are a part of that group, because most nonconformists have no religious connections to the church. 

      The biggest problem with criminals is that the crimes that they commit are irreversible; the crime will affect the victim for years into the future.
