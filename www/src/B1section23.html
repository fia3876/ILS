    • Sound


      sound - sonido



      I heard sounds outside and I opened the window to have a look Escuché sonidos fuera y abrí la ventana para echar un vistazo
      I thought it was a strange sound, but it was my brother playing guitar Pensé que era un ruido extraño, pero era mi hermano tocando la guitarra
      I sat and listened to the sound of the river all day Me senté y escuché el sonido del río todo el día
      I can hear the sound of the sea from my window Puedo escuchar el sonido del mar desde mi ventana

      noise - ruido

      I heard some noises downstairs so I got up to see what was happening Escuché algunos ruidos en la parte de abajo y me levanté para ver qué pasaba
      His car makes a strange noise. He needs to take it to the garage Su coche hace un ruido raro. Él necesita llevarlo al garaje
      The kids are making a lot of noise. Can you tell them to be quiet, please? Los niños están haciendo mucho ruido. ¿Puedes decirles que se calmen, por favor?
      Don't make noise, please. I'm trying to study in my room No hagas ruido, por favor. Estoy intentando estudiar en mi habitación

      noise - estruendo

      Oh, gosh! What an awful noise! I think the violin is not for you ¡Oh, dios! ¡Qué horrible ruido! Creo que el violín no es para ti
       

      The cars are making a terrible noise this morning and I have a headache Los coches están haciendo un horrible ruido esta mañana y tengo dolor de cabeza
      What's this noise? Sorry, mom, I broke some plates ¿Qué es este ruido? Lo siento, mamá, rompí algunos platos
      There was a terrible noise, so I went outside and saw a car had crashed Hubo un ruido horrible, así que salí fuera y vi que un coche se había estrellado

      racket - jaleo

      There was an amazing racket yesterday because we had won the match Hubo un jaleo sorprendente ayer porque habíamos ganado el partido
      What's this racket? I think the neighbors are having a party ¿Qué es este jaleo? Creo que los vecinos están haciendo una fiesta
      Your friends are making a terrible racket with their instruments Tus amigos están haciendo un horrible jaleo con sus instrumentos
      There is a terrible racket in the street because this pub is playing a concert Hay un horrible jaleo en la calle porque este pub está haciendo un concierto

      bang - estrepito , explosión

      We heard a bang because the door had closed in the wind Oímos un estrépito porque la puerta se había cerrado con el viento
      I heard some bangs when I finished the experiment Escuché algunas explosiones cuando terminé el experimento
      There was a bang when they tested the bomb Hubo un estrépito cuando ellos probaron la bomba
      We heard a bang and went outside; there had been an accident Oímos un estrépito y fuimos fuera; había habido un accidente


      rustle - susurro , crujido

      I heard the rustle of the leaves while I was walking in the forest Escuchaba el crujido de las hojas mientras caminaba por el bosque
      We didn't hear the movie because this guy was making a rustle with some bags No escuchábamos la película porque este tío estaba haciendo un crujido con algunas bolsas
      I heard the rustle of some papers while I was taking my test Escuché el crujido de algunos papeles mientras hacía mi examen
      Didn't you hear a rustle? Perhaps the window is open ¿No escuchaste un susurro? A lo mejor la ventana está abierta

    • The Discotheque

      Young people these days love to go to discotheques to dance all night. Discotheques are more attractive than pubs to young people because they love loud music and the excitement of all the noise. Rock and roll, heavy metal and punk are all examples of high volume music. Researchers have said that young people are damaging their hearing by listening to this kind of music at higher decibels than necessary, especially at concerts, where the noise level is extremely high. Some discos have shows that make a lot of racket, with bangs and pops from special effects, like explosions. You can even hear the noises when you are standing outside the discotheque; and if the disco is in the neighborhood of private residences or hotels, there are often complaints about the noise level. Many cities have started to control noise levels and even have machines that check the decibel level of the noise. If a disco is creating too much racket, they can be closed down. 

      I personally cannot stand the din of discotheques. I much prefer a nice quiet pub with a little bit of racket from the people chattering away about their problems and gossip. Pubs can be noisy too, but not as noisy as a disco or a concert. Why do people like noise so much? Noise excites people and makes them feel energetic and alive. Noise can create energy, but noise can also control mood and anxiety. For example, there are many CDs that you can buy that are not music at all, but the sounds of water falling or leaves that are rustling in the wind. These sounds are very tranquil and some people like to listen to the sounds to relax and meditate. My favorite sound is the sound of palm trees rustling in the wind because it relaxes me. There is something about the rustling of the palm trees that puts me to sleep. Whenever I feel anxious or depressed I like to listen to a waterfall or leaves rustling in order to relax. It makes me feel comfortable and calm. Soft music is also nice and can be good for meditation, especially classical music that is not loud. 

      Of course, silence is also a good way to clear your mind and meditate. You know what they say, silence is golden and maybe silence is the best sound of all.
