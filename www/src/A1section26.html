    • Grammar

    •   La conjugación inglesa: El Presente Simple afirmativo

      En inglés, la conjugación de cualquier verbo que no sea semi-auxiliar como To be y To Have Got consta de cuatro formas verbales para el presente, cuatro para el pasado, cuatro para el futuro y cuatro para el condicional:

                   Presente           Pasado           Fururo           Condicional

               Simple              Simple            Simple             Simple

                 Continuo          Continuo         Continuo         Continuo

                   Perfecto           Perfecto           Perfecto            Perfecto

                     Perf. Cont.        Perf. Cont.       Perf. Cont.       Perf. Cont. 


      Empezaremos estudiando los tiempos simples:

      Presente Simple

      Si queremos conjugar el verbo To drink en presente simple:


      drink beber


      Para los pronombres I, you, we, they simplemente quitamos la partícula "to” al infinitivo:


      I drink Yo bebo


      Para los pronombres she, he, it quitamos la partícula "to” y además añadimos una -s al verbo:


      She drinks Ella bebe


      De esta manera conjugaremos el verbo To drink como sigue:



      I drik Yo bebo

      You drink Tú bebes

      He drinks Él bebe

      She drinks Ella bebe

      It drinks Ello bebe

      We drink Nosotros bebemos

      You drink Ustedes beben

      They drink Ellos beben

      Para


      Utilizamos la preposición "for" con el significado en español "para":



      This cat is for Peter Este gato es para Peter

      I drink beer for lunch Yo bebo cerveza para almorzar

      He makes dinner for his wife Él prepara la cena para su mujer

      Jan buys a book for you Jan compra un libro para ti


      Con el mismo significado podemos usar la preposición "to":



      I study to learn Yo estudio para aprender

      She buys toys for ken Ella le compra juguetes a Ken

      Lucy cooks for her husband on friday Lucy cocina para su marido los viernes

    • Reading

    • Reading: Lee el texto detenidamente. Puede que en el texto figuren palabras que todavía no se han estudiado, para estos casos aconsejamos el uso de un diccionario. Después de leer el texto varias veces, responde las preguntas que figuran del mismo.


    • My mom wakes up my sister, brother and I very early every day. She has an alarm clock that she puts in each of our rooms to wake us up at the same time. The alarm on the alarm clock always rings at 6:30 am. After I get out of bed, I get dressed into my school uniform. I always put my nametag on, so people know my name is Sarah. Then I walk downstairs to the kitchen. I meet my sister Jen and my brother Tom there. They are already dressed and ready for school. I sit between Jen and Tom every morning at the table. I drink orange juice. Jen and Tom drink grape juice. The three of us eat eggs and bacon for breakfast. When we finish breakfast, we walk outside to Dad's car. Dad meets us outside. Tom sits in front with Dad. Jen and I sit behind Dad and Tom in the back seat. Dad drives us to school. Tom walks behind Jen and I as we enter the school. Tom's classroom is on the third floor. Jen's classroom is on the first floor next to my classroom.

      The name of Jen's teacher is Mrs. Daniels. Mrs. Daniels' classroom is the biggest classroom in the school. All day long Mrs. Daniels reads to the class.

      My teacher is Mr. Harris. Mr. Harris does not read to my class. Instead he tells us to study for our exams. At 3:30 pm, it is time for everyone in school to go home. Jen, Tom, and I wait outside for Dad to finish work and pick us up. Dad works ten miles from our school. We play soccer everyday at this time as we wait for Dad to pick us up. Dad arrives and we get into the car. Tom always sits in front with Dad. We get home twenty minutes later. Mom has dinner ready for us. I wash my hands and then eat. After dinner, Tom, Jen and I watch T.V. At 8 pm we go to bed.

    • Lee el texto y decide si las frases son verdaderas (T) o falsas (F).
