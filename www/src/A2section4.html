    • Grammar

    •  Easily confused words

      sensitive - sensible

      sensitive sensible
      sensible sensato, razonable

      She is a very sensitive person. She cries easily Ella es una persona muy sensible. Ella llora fácilmente
      Be sensible! Driving too fast is crazy ¡Sé sensato! Conducir demasiado rápido es una locura

      actually - currently

      actually precisamente, de hecho
      currently actualmente


      Actually, I'm not happy with the job De hecho, no estoy feliz con el trabajo
      They are living in Brighton currently Ellos están viviendo en Brighton actualmente

      friend - ally - partner - colleague - comrade - companion - mate - pal - buddy

      friend amigo
      ally aliado
      partner pareja, compañero
      colleague colega, compañero
      comrade camarada

      Mate, pal y buddy en un contexto informal

      buddy compañero, colega
      pal colega, amigo


      John is a friend of mine John es mi amigo
      France was a British ally in Second World War Francia fue un aliado británico en la Segunda Guerra Mundial
      I play soccer at school on Friday. On my team, my partners are really funny Yo juego al fútbol en el colegio cada viernes. En mi equipo, mis compañeros son muy divertidos
      I work for Madison & Co. It's an important international firm. Henry is my colleague, he also works there Yo trabajo para Madison & Co. Es una empresa internacional importante. Henry es mi colega, él también trabaja allí
      Comrades, we need to call an election and choose a new president Camaradas, necesitamos hacer unas elecciones y elegir un nuevo presidente
      Hi pals! Do you feel like a drink tonight? ¡Hola colegas! ¿Tienen ganas de una copa esta noche?

      worried - nervous - apprenhensive - anxious - scared

      worried preocupado
      nervous nervioso
      apprehensive aprensivo
      anxious ansioso
      scared asustado


      She was really nervous because it was her first performance Ella estaba realmente nerviosa porque era su primera actuación
      I'm worried about Lucy. It's very late and she isn't at home Estoy preocupado por Lucy. Es muy tarde y ella no está en casa
      She doesn't want to travel, she is apprehensive about that flight Ella no quiere viajar, ella está inquieta con ese vuelo
      I'm anxious because I need to move to another house and I don't know how to do it Estoy ansioso porque necesito mudarme a otra casa y no sé cómo hacerlo
      I'm scared of spiders. They make me sick Estoy asustada de las arañas. Me enferman

    • Reading: Lee el texto detenidamente. Puede que en el texto figuren palabras que todavía no se han estudiado, para estos casos aconsejamos el uso de un diccionario. Después de leer el texto varias veces, responde las preguntas que figuran del mismo.


       A Tourist in a Foreign Land


      I have been travelling alone for a week now. I plan to visit Russia, Asia, the United States and Australia. I am a little bit anxious about travelling in Russia and Asia because of the languages. I am worried that it will be difficult to communicate with the people that I meet. Since I love meeting people and making friends, communication is important to me. I was going to make this journey with a colleague from work, but he is in the hospital. Nothing serious, but the doctor said it was impossible for him to travel. 

      I have got some books that will help me with the language problem. There is so much information in these books, that it makes me apprehensive that I can remember the words that I need. I started with the words for"friend” in the other languages. I discovered that each region has their own casual expression for the word "friend!” In the United States people say "buddy” or "pal.” "Hey, buddy, would you like a beer?” Actually, that is only used when both friendsare men. One expression that is confusing is that many couples that are not married use the expression "partner” for their girlfriend or boyfriend. "My partner works at the bank.” I do not know why they cannot say "My boyfriend works at the bank.” It does not sound sensible to me. 

      In Australia they use"mate,” similar to England. In Russia, "comrade” is a good expression for a friend. I am currently in Russia this week, so I am using that expression a lot. Next week I am going to Asia. I have not even looked at the book on Chinese, yet! I think I am scared of all of those pictures that they use for words!
